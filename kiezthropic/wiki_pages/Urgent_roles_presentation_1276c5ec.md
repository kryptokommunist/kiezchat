# Urgent roles presentation

## Problem Statement

We have the smallest board this year that we've ever had. We're struggling to get realizers for this year and it's becoming a difficult situation.

At the end of February, if we haven't filled many of these roles we may need to reconsider doing the event this year. We will do an evaluation then.

## Resolving the Problem

We can encourage more enagagement at Burn Night to try to find more people that would be interested to take on a realizer role. The Arbeitsamt is a small group with a lot of tasks and won't be able to tackle this alone.

## Details

If you have connections to people that you think may be interested in taking on one of the following roles, please tell them about it and encourage them. Bring them to Villa K if they are interested! The website contains information, so does discord, and the newsletter. Also the wiki.

People can also learn more by starting by reading about the principles or the German burner website.

## Key takeaways (Based on session audio recording)

#### **The State of Realizers**

* **"Low Part of the Wave":** There is a general lack of engagement currently. This is attributed to "real world" stress and the difficulty of survival making people less available for unpaid roles.
* **The February Deadline:** The end of February is as the critical point to assess if the event has enough organizational support to move forward effectively.

#### **Organizational Strategy**

* **Decentralization:** The "Quest Office" (Arbeitsamt) cannot be run by just two people. Every department (Welfare, Security, etc.) needs to help manage its own recruitment.
* **Personal Connection:** Reaching out to "gems" (talented individuals) from previous years via phone calls or 1-on-1 meetings is more effective than generic Discord pings.
* **The "Playbook" System:** Many roles have "Starter Packs" (templates for emails, forms, and shift plans), making it easier for new people to step into leadership without starting from scratch.

#### **Key Open Roles & Departments**

* **DWO (Default World Office):** Needs German speakers to handle bureaucracy, permits, insurance, and fire safety.
* **Critical Infrastructure:** Forklift drivers (licensed), Site Planning, and Power/Water realizers are high priorities.
* **Care & Safety:** Welfare, Consent, and Harm Reduction leads are still needed.
* **Spark On-Site Weekend:** Planning for late April to allow camps and artists to visualize the space.

#### **Recruitment & Onboarding Tips**

* **Principles First:** When explaining Kiez Burn to newcomers, start with the 10 Principles rather than the logistics.
* **The "Myth of Completion":** Many community members assume the team is "full" or that the event runs itself. Leaders need to actively dispel this myth to encourage new people to step up.
* **Low-Bar Entry:** Encourage people to join the Villa K hangouts or take small shifts at BurnHalla to get a feel for the community.

#### **Action Items**

* **Villa K Hangouts:** Wednesday (maybe gonna be Thursdays soon?) meetings to facilitate 1-on-1 recruitment.
* **Quest Office at BurnHalla:** A physical station to sign people up for roles and shifts. (Yay - I think this worked so well!)
* **Circle Time:** Departmental teams need to meet to establish communication channels and meeting calendars.
