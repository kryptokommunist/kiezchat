## Description

This year we've been given the opportunity to expand from our humble beginnings preaching out of a geodesic dome into something much bigger, much grander… more numinous: the large stage at the back of the forest.

***The Cathedral of Crucifera will be a fractal sanctuary truly worthy of the reverence that our goddess has cultivated over the seasons.*** 

White, gold, and green fabrics close off the front. Our 'stained glass' window hangs in the centre. Her image beckons those exploring the depth of the forest to come, peel back the door and venture into this house of worship. Sunlight streams through the window and onto the altar in the center. Our saints stand on either side, keeping watch of the plate where pilgrims leave their offerings.

Our chandelier, a 1.5m wide romanesco filled with over 250 individually programmable LEDs, hangs from the ceiling (with very safe chain-based hanging mechanisms we're currently designing that integrates with the current chain-based chains in the roof). Beanbags, cushions, blankets provide comfort for the weary. A confession box, a divination station, and a zen garden provide absolution, guidance, and peace to those who seek it.

During the day, we will provide services and offer the space to others to perform their rituals/workshops. At night, the sanctuary becomes somewhere for a different kind of service: resting, reflecting, recharging - with crispy vegetable refreshments, LEDs galore, and projections onto the walls.

Join us under Her leaves (and now a much larger roof).

## Environmental Impact and LNT

Everything that we purchase we intend to reuse, both at next Kiezburns and also our own events and retreats. Most fabrics will be altered with light and layering rather than painting - in case the expansion of the church next year requires adjustments to be made. We even have an incredible track record with maintaining and repairing our fairy lights. We will be reusing a lot of our own resources from the church last year. \n

Largest moop potential comes from damage to the fake leaves and them leaving their vines as a result, people taking fruit / vegetables and dropping them, rogue staples/nails we use to secure things. Some of the fake plants already exist and we've been using them for 3 last years. We also plan to print a variety of decorations - so some plastic generation, but we've reused everything so far.

## Safety and Accessibility 

* The chandelier and window will be mounted from the ceiling. We're tasked our best engineers with coming up with the best way to do this - will be utilising existing mounting points in the ceiling.
* Accessibility wise: you need to climb onto the stage which will make it difficult for people with mobility issues. There are stairs at the back through a secret door, we might be able to make some sort of ramp?
