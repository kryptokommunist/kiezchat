# Description

## Summary

The Vibecycle is a mobile, interactive music art piece: a cargo bike with an electric piano, battery-powered sound, reactive lights, and enough rainbow energy to accidentally turn a random path into a small parade.

Last year at Kiez Burn it was a huge success. It rolled around the playa, picked up singers and dancers, and turned little moments into spontaneous concerts. The Vibecycle was also present at the European Leadership Summit in Nijmegen this year, where it continued its strange but beautiful mission of making people sing in places where they did not necessarily expect to sing. It was endorsed and enthusiastically received by many European burners there, as well as by Burning Man Project staff, which is a sentence I am still trying to say without grinning like an idiot.

The core idea stays the same: one person pedals, one person plays piano, and everyone around is invited to join. This upgrade turns the Vibecycle from a mobile piano karaoke bike into a more open rolling jam platform.

In short: less phone staring, fewer cables, more shared singing, more ridiculous rolling piano karaoke.

## What Changes This Year?

The existing Vibecycle already has the cargo bike, piano, sound system, battery, LEDs, and software basis. This application is not asking to rebuild the whole thing. It is asking for a focused upgrade:

### 1. Wireless instrument input for jam sessions

 ![Bose S1 Pro Plus Instrument Transmitter](/api/attachments.redirect?id=beaa7ab7-3aa8-43eb-8976-e0de91435735)

The Bose S1 Pro Plus Instrument Transmitter would allow someone with a guitar or another instrument with a 6.3 mm jack output to join the Vibecycle wirelessly. This makes the Vibecycle less of a piano-only object and more of a small rolling jam platform.

### 2. LED lyrics and chord display

 ![Sketch showing LED matrix placement on the Vibecycle](/api/attachments.redirect?id=2e8d3111-a1b4-4e09-816a-445f28cd3dd1)

The matrix panels mount low on the front of the Vibecycle, facing singers and musicians. Best case is three panels; two or one still works if funding is limited, but three is where the display starts feeling like a real shared song/chord surface instead of a very determined calculator.

The display can show lyrics, chorus prompts, song titles, and chord progressions, so people can join without phone-squinting or guessing chords by ear. The goal is shared information for shared music.

 ![64x32 RGB LED Matrix from BerryBase - 6mm Raster, front](/api/attachments.redirect?id=aa06b614-0549-42ed-9bfe-91b70001ff8a)

The matrix also needs the small electronics around it: wires, buttons, connectors, and control bits. Without those, the panels are just expensive shiny rectangles with opinions.

### 3. Second wireless microphone input

 ![Bose S1 Pro Plus Mic/Line TX](/api/attachments.redirect?id=4707fc9d-6e61-4085-a26b-6a9dd639c50b)

I already own one Bose S1 Pro Plus Mic/Line transmitter. A second one would allow two people to sing karaoke together while the piano is playing. Duets, call-and-response, emotional power ballads, badly remembered ABBA harmonies - the important stuff.

## Singing Circle / Karaoke Mode

The Vibecycle continues the tradition of the Kiez Burn Singing Circle and the digital songbook, now through the Vibecycle app at https://vibecycle.fun/. The app remains the songbook; the LED display makes the key lyrics shared and visible, so the group can look up and sing together instead of disappearing into phones.

The result should feel like a moving piano karaoke bar that can roll up to Harlot House, stop somewhere on the playa, collect a few voices, and then disappear again like a musically questionable rainbow ghost.

## Jam Session Mode

The Vibecycle is not only for karaoke. It is also for musicians who want to join in without turning the whole thing into a cable spaghetti sacrifice.

With the instrument transmitter, someone can plug in a guitar or another mono instrument and play through the Bose S1 Pro Plus while staying mobile. This makes it easier to have spontaneous sessions with piano, voice, and another instrument, especially in places where setting up a full PA would be absurd.

The LED matrix can also show chord progressions to all musicians at once. That moves jams beyond the same safe four-chord loop and makes them more inclusive for people who cannot easily hear the changes or watch another player's hands.

# Environmental Impact and LNT

The environmental approach is mostly: reuse the weird thing I already built instead of building a new weird thing.

The cargo bike, piano, Bose S1 Pro Plus, battery system, existing LEDs, microcontrollers, and software setup are already available and will be reused. The new items are durable electronics intended to stay with the Vibecycle and be used again at future Kiez Burns, micro burns, decompressions, and similar community events.

LNT plan:

* No single-use materials are part of the art concept.
* Cable ties, wire offcuts, tape, packaging, and broken tiny technical sadness will be packed out.
* The LED matrix and transmitters will be mounted/removable in a way that does not shed parts while moving.
* Electronics and batteries will not be abandoned, disposed of on site, or left in the care of the forest spirits.
* If any electronic part reaches end-of-life later, it will go to proper electronics recycling.

Power usage exists, but the system is battery powered. Charging can happen away from peak power times, and the total power draw stays small compared with many fixed installations.

# Safety and Accessibility

## Art Vehicle Safety

The Vibecycle is human-powered and intentionally slow. It is not easy to ride, so it will be operated carefully and slowly, at roughly countryside walking speed. Riders will be instructed not to go fast, especially near people, camps, or dancing humans with limited situational awareness.

The bike is primarily used on paths, runway, concrete, and stable ground. It is not meant for rough terrain. If the playa is too crowded, too muddy, or otherwise unsuitable, it can simply stop and become a stationary piano bar.

## LED Matrix Safety

The LED matrix will be mounted securely and tested before the burn. It will not be placed where it blocks the rider's view or creates sharp corners at face height. Cables will be routed and fixed so they do not catch on people, wheels, or enthusiastic capes.

Brightness will be kept reasonable, especially at night. The goal is readability and vibe, not blinding the innocent.

## Sound Safety

The sound system is a Bose S1 Pro Plus. It is suitable for small performances and singalongs, not a massive sound camp situation. Volume will be kept appropriate to location and time, and quiet hours will be respected.

## Accessibility

The shared display improves accessibility by making the important song information visible to everyone nearby. A passer-by can just look up and sing.

The Vibecycle can also come to people instead of requiring everyone to go to a fixed stage. It creates small pop-up performances for people who might not seek out a scheduled show.

# Power usage estimate

The Vibecycle is battery powered. The old estimate was around 147W while running piano, amplification, electronics, and LED rainbow. The LED matrix will add some power draw, but it can be brightness-limited and does not need to run at full-white maximum power because this is a lyric display, not a portable artificial sun.

Conservative estimated running consumption after the upgrade:

| Component | Estimated Power |
|----|---:|
| LED rainbow / decorative lights | 72W |
| LED lyric matrix, brightness-limited | 30-80W |
| E-piano | 25W |
| Bose S1 Pro Plus / amplification | 40W |
| Electronics / controllers / networking | 10-15W |
| Wireless transmitters | internal rechargeable batteries |
| **Estimated total while active** | **177-232W** |

The existing battery capacity is around 40Ah x 12V, roughly 512Wh before losses. That means a typical active session should still be manageable from battery power. Charging will happen when needed and can be timed for power dips.

The Bose transmitters have their own integrated rechargeable batteries and can be charged via USB-C or in the Bose S1 Pro Plus charging slots. They are specified for up to 6 hours of runtime.
