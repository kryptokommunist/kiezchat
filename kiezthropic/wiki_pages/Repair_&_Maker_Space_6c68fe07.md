## Description

### Vision

Imagine a world where things lasted for longer time. Nothing tears, falls apart, flickers, rusts, catches on fire, or disappears (especially nothing gets broken or lost at burns). 

While the utopian world doesn't exist yet, our Maker Space brings that philosophy to life. 

Hosted at BASH Camp, we are providing the infrastructure, platform, materials, and community knowledge to make mending and up-cycling a core piece of Kiez Burn culture. At our repair and maker space we will create a place where things are repaired instead of thrown away, repurposed instead of replaced. A space where burners learn and teach each other how to repair their tents themselves, mend a flickering LED, build a community, and fix a broken heart. A space where people can gain practical DIY skills, share knowledge, borrow ideas, and help each other make things last longer, while giving new life to unused materials. 

We're bringing a repair & maker space to the playa: workshops, skill-sharing, repair stations, open creative time, labeling tools (to tag personal and camp equipment). Through this, we bring to reality several key principles: **Radical Self-reliance** (mending our own gear), **Radical Participation** (learning together), and **Leave No Trace** (preventing broken items from becoming trash). Including a simple future hope: that this year, thanks to our equipment tagging workshop, the identical blue Decathlon towel we all left near the sauna might actually find its way back to us.

### Tachles

Our dream serves as a platform enabling other burners to fulfill the 11 principles. It blends open-floor creative time with structured, community-led programming. Our confirmed curriculum spans technical, creative, and community-building workshops, including:

We will have a maker space and repair place officially open at least 3 hours per day (more on demand). In addition, we will host at least 2 workshops per day. Most workshops will be organized by camp members, but we are open to guest workshops (2 currently confirmed). These combine skill sharing, community building, fun, and food making. 


### **Why fund this hardware request:** 

Unlike other dreams, the tools and learning how to use them *is* the experience and the dream itself. We are not asking for personal tools to construct our own camp infrastructure or build an art installation. Instead, this grant funds the shared interactive hardware needed to run the public workshops. 

To ensure full compliance with funding guidelines, we will permanently label all purchased equipment as Kiezburn Dream Inventory from day one. We will return everything directly to the Kiezburn inventory at strike or once the transport returns to Berlin, and we are happy to run post-burn workshops on how to use them safely.

### Specific Workshops Include

* **Technical & Repair:** Soldering, repairing, Sewing, Equipment Tagging, Food hacking.
* **Creative & Making:** Zine making, Block painting, Body painting, and Recycled decoration.
* **Community & Wellbeing:** Intro for new burners, CPR training, Morning stretching, brining the burn  spirit into the default world

## Environmental Impact and LNT

We are sustainability-focused. Materials will include recycled and repurposed items such as old electronics, leftover acrylic sheets, textile waste from upcycling clothing, and leftover vegetable materials. A key part of the project is teaching burners practical upcycling and repair skills they can apply in their daily lives.

All waste will be minimized, upcycled when possible, separated, and taken back or recycled properly, following Leave No Trace principles.

Most materials are non-degradable. Any unused materials will be stored and reused at other Kiezburn-related events or at the next Kiezburn.

## Safety and Accessibility 

We have strong experience in safety through our leading roles in a Berlin makerspace. Our camp includes a risk assessment expert, two (almost three) emergency medical paramedics, and a licensed construction engineer.

The space will be designed to be safe and inclusive: wheelchair accessible when ground conditions allow, English-speaking, FLINTA-friendly, and mindful of ADHD and autistic accessibility needs.
