## Description

Be the Fish or See the Fish is an immersive, participatory aquarium installation where visitors must choose between two roles:


The Observer — watching from outside the aquarium

The Fish — entering the installation as part of the exhibit itself


Participants who choose to "be the fish" select a fish archetype, costume, or marine persona and become living performers inside the aquarium environment. Some fish are seductive, strange, territorial, glamorous, lonely, dangerous, decorative, invasive, endangered, hypersexualized, camouflaged, or absurd. Every fish reflects a human social role.


The audience outside the aquarium becomes the observer, consumer, voyeur, tourist, judge, admirer, scientist, predator, or customer.


The piece playfully explores the tension between:

performer and audience

autonomy and display

voyeurism and participation

consumption and identity

spectacle and consent

freedom and social conditioning


It is intentionally funny, awkward, visually ridiculous, and slightly uncomfortable.


Participants always have agency: nobody is assigned a role. Everyone chooses whether they want to watch or be watched.
