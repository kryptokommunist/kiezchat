## Key takeways (Based on the session audio recording)

##  (Maybe some redundancy with above)

* #### **1. Communication and Information Sharing**
  * **Channel Fatigue:** Recognition that the community has too many channels (Discord, Telegram, Wiki, Email). The focus should be on making one source the "default."
  * **Engaging Formats:** Using TikTok-style videos or short clips from realizers to explain roles and lower the intimidation factor of volunteering.

  #### **2. Incentives and Swag (Physical Rewards)**
  * **The Power of Swag:** Proposed physical rewards (badges, sweaters, patches) to allow volunteers to show-off their contributions.
  * **Internal Sourcing:** Utilizing community members with screen-printing or pin-making skills to keep costs low.
  * **Limited Editions:** e.g., at Russian burns, Nevada regional —> medals or high-tier swag for veteran volunteers or those performing difficult shifts (e.g., night shifts).
  * **DIY Swag:** Hosting workshops at Villa K to DIY rewards, i.e., social pre-event engagement.

  #### **3. Recruitment and Personal Connection**
  * **The "Volunteer Lead" Role:** Suggestion to have a dedicated person per department whose only job is to socialize, organize picnics, and create WhatsApp groups for volunteers to make them feel seen.
  * **Co-Leads and Mentorship:** Encouraging a "Watch Me" approach where new people shadow experienced leads without taking on full responsibility immediately.
  * **Personal Connection:** Consensus that 1-on-1 recruitment ("Hey, you'd be great at this") is far more effective than general call-outs on social media.

  #### **4. Operational and Sign-up Improvements**
  * **Ticket Integration:** Re-implementing a system where ticket buyers must indicate areas of interest or receive a direct link to the shift sign-up platform immediately upon purchase.
  * **Meal Vouchers:** Using the volunteer kitchen as a direct incentive for hard-to-fill shifts (e.g., Friday night Rangers).
  * **Overbooking:** Strategies to recruit double the necessary volunteers for a shift to ensure the workload remains chill and manageable, even if someone drops out.

  #### **5. Cultural Rebranding (Gamification)**
  * **The Quest Office:** Rebranding the Arbeitsamt to the "Quest Office" to make volunteering feel like an RPG experience.
  * **Lowering Barriers:** Introducing "Low Entry" tasks like the "Lamp Lighters" or champagne "LNT Trash Walks" that require no sign-up and foster a habit of participation.
  * **Gate Engagement:** Encouraging Gate staff to playfully check if participants have signed up for shifts as they enter the event.
