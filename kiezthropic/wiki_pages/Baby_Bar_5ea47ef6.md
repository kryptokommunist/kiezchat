## Description

Get ready to meet *The Baby Bar*, a mobile art cart that's part quirky house, part unexpected experience. Measuring 1.20m long and 0.70m wide, this house on wheels is designed to spark curiosity and delight wherever it rolls.

From the outside, it looks like a whimsical, miniature home—complete with windows just big enough for your head or hands. Stick them in, and you'll be served a drink in the most unconventional way imaginable.

But what's inside? That's where the real magic happens. The interior is a world of wonder, brimming with eccentric surprises, peculiar inhabitants, and playful interactions. Without giving too much away, let's just say this house is *alive*. Expect lights, sounds, and maybe even some unexpected conversations with the residents.

Whether you're thirsty, curious, or just passing by, *The Baby Bar* promises a little dose of mischief and a lot of fun. Come see it roll through KiezBurn and discover what's waiting behind the windows.

## Environmental Impact

We have built the bar in a way that it can be easily re-decorated or altered for years to come. The baby Bar has been to both KB 25 and Hamburn 25 so far. We also plan to bring it to the next BurnHalla and future Kiezburns. 

The decorations were mostly made from pre-existing screws, fabric scaps, paints and dolls from Kleinanzeigen. The wood and cart was funded by last years Dream. This year we would love to improve a few things, plus transporting the bar from Berlin costs money. 

The Baby Bar is not Moopy in any way and is stored in a basement in Berlin throughout the year.
