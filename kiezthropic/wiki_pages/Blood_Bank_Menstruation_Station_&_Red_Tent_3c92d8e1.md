# 🩸 Blood Bank: Menstruation Station & Red Tent

At Kiez Burn, menstruation remains largely invisible - yet for many of us, it's a central, embodied experience. Our Dream is to create a two-part sanctuary: a practical and private *Menstrual Changing Station* for cleaning cups, discs, or pads, and a soothing *Womb Tent* for rest, ritual, and connection. Existing compost toilets are to small and offer no privacy or facilities for managing menstrual care with dignity and ease. This space not only acknowledges the cyclical nature of bleeding bodies and honors menstruation not as an inconvenience, but as natural and something to be celebrated. By hosting daily *Womb Circles* and offering space for reflection and ritual, we aim to reclaim menstruation as something powerful, communal, and visible at the Burn. This is a space we've always needed, but never had.

A survey of 102 Kiez Burn festival attendees from February 2026 reveals a clear need: 92% of respondents support the establishment of a dedicated menstruation room at the event. 75 people stated that they attend festivals despite menstruating, 49 of whom have already menstruated at Kiez Burn. 80 people reported significant difficulties: lack of access to clean water, overcrowded and unhygienic toilets, and physical discomfort without a place to retreat were the most frequently cited problems. Many feel forced to switch to disposable products, even though they normally use menstrual cups. The planned room is intended to fulfill three core needs: practical infrastructure (water station, hygienic changing area), a quiet place to rest (womb tent with hot water bottles, tea, and support), and raising awareness around bleeding bodies in a creative way to destigmatize and reclaim menstruation.

At a central, easy to reach location at the Burn and for different points during a bleed—whether it's the sudden start of a period, needing to urgently empty a full menstrual cup and cooking it clean with hot water, or simply wanting a moment of privacy to manage discomfort—the *Menstrual Changing Station* and *Womb Tent* provides both kinds of sanctuary. Whether it's the practical need for water and hygiene, or the emotional need for rest and grounding, our space is designed to support menstruators in any moment of their cycle with dignity, calm, and care. This is more than a toilet. It is a place that conveys and celebrates the beauty of nature's cycle with a beautiful design and a beautiful awareness campaign at Kiezburn. We will provide printed info all over the festival grounds and host two daily formats around bodywork and womb circles as suggested in our schedule below. Also, the place will be accessible on self-service 24 hours a day.

At Garbicz, Melt and Immergut Festivals, spaces for menstruating bodies already belong to the festival infrastructure. We believe that Kiezburn should have the most active, beautiful and integrated one and that it also should be a festival standard to make sure that everyone can have a good time and feel at ease. We believe this to be an integral part of the future festival structure but for the first time we would love to fund this as an Art Dream to ensure it is especially visible, creative and expressive.

## Description

We imagine two complementary spaces to fulfill this dream - one space - *Womb Tent* - to rest, relax and share about cycle/menstruation/emotions and a second space - *Menstrual Changing Station* - to provide a private, safe and hygienic space for bleeding bodies to change menstrual cups/tampons etc. with access to running water and hygiene products.

Below is a detailed description for each of the two dreamed spaces:

***Womb Ten*t: Cosy tent to rest, relax and share about cycle/menstruation/emotions**

**Purpose:** To create a calm, cosy, resting space for bleeding bodies during their menstruation/bleeding phase at Kiezburn. The purpose of this space is to make all bleeding bodies feel safe, respected and allowing them to feel whatever they have to feel in the moment (pain, emotions, feelings etc).

**What we aim to provide:** In this space we aim to provide a calm, sacred and respectful atmosphere with subtle smells, calming music, space to rest and lie down, space to share in circle with other bleeding bodies (daily events we aim to organize, see Table below) and a space with some information in the form of books or cards to inform on the nature, teachings and magic of the menstrual cycle.

24 hours open to women who bleed to have a space to rest, surrounded by soft sounds.

***Menstrual Changing Station*: Hygiene space**

**Purpose**: To create a space for bleeding bodies to empty menstrual cups, change tampons and allow them to clean themselves in a private atmosphere with running water and provided hygiene products.

**What we aim to provide:** In this space, we aim to provide a closed, private, clean and calm space with running water in the form of a sink or shower, a space for the disposal of menstrual blood. We further aim to provide all desired hygiene products for bleeding bodies including wipes, paper towels, tampons, slip inlays, biodegradable soap and intimate soap as well as desinfecting and room spray.

Menstrual care often involves rinsing cups or cloth pads, refreshing intimate areas, or simply having access to warm water to soothe cramps or clean oneself more thoroughly. Therefore, access to **hot water** and a **high standard of cleanliness** is not just practical—it is essential. We commit to maintaining the space with **two daily cleaning shifts** and by ensuring the availability of biodegradable soap, disinfectants, and fresh supplies.

## Existing funds

We already have a number of materials to support the realization of this Dream. For the *Womb Tent*, we have a suitable tent structure, a small collection of books to start a menstruation-focused library, and some fairy lights to create a calming atmosphere. We are also in contact with potential partners—such as **La Blutique** or **Einhorn**—to supply menstrual products and educational materials. While we still require funding for infrastructure and hygiene supplies, these existing contributions give us a strong foundation to build from.

Since we received the feedback that the tent will probably not be funded, we aim to create an alternative structure for the Menstruation Changing Stations with the white room dividers and possibly some tarpes to protect the space against the rain.

## Power usage estimate

How much power are you going to use and for what?

* **Lighting:** One permanent low-energy LED light in the *Menstrual Changing Station* to ensure it is usable 24/7, plus battery-powered or solar fairy lights for ambiance in the *Womb Tent*.
* **Power Source:** Minimal power needs; we aim to use solar where possible or connect to a low-draw communal grid.
* **Water:** Running water will be provided via a garden hose hookup. We are also exploring a low-tech hot water solution (e.g. solar-heated canister or black water bag system) to allow for more comfort during washing.
* **No electrical appliances.**
