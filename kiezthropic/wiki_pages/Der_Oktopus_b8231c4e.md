## Description

Der Oktopus is a \~5-metre wooden octopus with one mantle (head) and **eight arms, each shaped in the aesthetic of a different community that makes up...the Octopus.** What will come of the eight arms? What communities will they represent? That's a question we're holding open 😉 They radiate from a mantle that lights up at night and lets burners climb inside in pairs.

The arms are an open invitation. **Two have confirmed Tentacle Leads, two are in active discussion, four are open** — to be co-authored through **Berlin pre-build prototyping sessions (Jun 9–17)** and the **on-site Deco track during build week** — open to anyone who shows up to shape one. The octopus's body is finished by the same many-handed effort the metaphor describes: distributed intelligence, eight minds in one body, soft and shape-shifting, every arm semi-autonomous but bound to the whole. When she burns, the communities who showed up watch their arm return to the same fire.

On nights, a **metachrosis layer** — live VJ projection on the mantle — animates the skin so the octopus "camouflages" through the visual languages of the communities she carries. (Live, on-the-night only; removed before burn-down.)

## Environmental Impact and LNT

### Environmental Impact

* **Materials:** Almost entirely **untreated, dry pine** from regional sawmill [kantholz.tf](http://kantholz.tf) (Teltow-Fläming, \~70 km from site). Cladding: 25 mm Schwartenbretter (slab boards) and B-grade pine. Carved features: 40 mm Bohlen pine. **No treated wood, no synthetic paints (acrylic / alkyd explicitly excluded), no high-ember materials** (paper, cardboard, bushes). Tentacle Leads explicitly briefed to source decorative materials second-hand where possible.
* **Finishes.** **Water-based natural paints only** — lime wash (Kalkfarbe), clay paint (Lehmfarbe), casein, mineral / iron-oxide pigments.
* **Carbon footprint.** The burn consumes approximately **4.2 m³ of dry pine (\~2,100 kg)** Combustion releases roughly **3.9 t of biogenic CO₂** — carbon the trees absorbed during growth, treated as carbon-neutral over the forestry cycle ([kantholz.tf](http://kantholz.tf) is a small regional sawmill with regrowth in pace). The fossil-attributable footprint (sawmill processing energy, transport, fasteners, electronics) is on the order of **100–250 kg CO₂** . We will offset this via a nominal contribution to [Wren](https://www.wren.co/) project after the burn.

### LNT Plan

**Ground anchoring.** \~50 cm rebar stakes (Bewehrungsstahl), driven through the base frame — recovered and reused post-burn.

**MOOP & weather.** All cladding panels overlapped and screwed (no gap detailing). Exterior non-climbable, so interaction is channeled away from fragile decorative surfaces. All electrical weatherproof-rated, dry-weather operation only, removed before burn-down.

**Strike plan.** The mantle's tipi-style column arrangement is designed to **collapse inward in place** as columns char roughly together. Arms are detachable: During burn prep, the arms will be discreetly detached from the mantle. Once the mantle has collapsed and flame height drops below \~1 m, the team rearranges the arms back into the remaining fire so the whole structure burns in place. Ashes cleared, rebar and fasteners recovered, dedicated *Effigy Strike* shifts already scheduled on [signup.com](http://signup.com) (Jun 28).

**Reuse & recycling.** Lighting, microcontrollers, Unused timber and paint offered as **Kiez Burn** shared inventory candidates after the burn.

## Safety and Accessibility 

* **Audience perimeter:** 3× mantle apex height (\~15 m radius from the head). Arm portions under 1 m do not factor into the perimeter — agreed with Ole 2026-04-04.
* **Interior access during festival:** climbable, **max 2 burners at any time**, internal frame designed for foot/hand interaction. Closed and cleared before any fuel application.
* **Exterior:** not climbable; no public access to upper outer surfaces.
* **Active electrical** (LEDs, microcontrollers, fog, lasers): all weatherproof low-voltage, dry-weather operation only, all removed before burn. Fog machine and laser projector live in a **partitioned cavity inside the mantle** not accessible to visiting burners. Laser beams exit through bounded openings aimed at an external surface — no beam crosses the public interior; no beam at audience eye height in the surrounding area.
* **Night lighting:** mantle internally and externally lit throughout each night with weatherproof low-voltage LEDs.
* **Collapse:** redundant tipi-style columns → expected inward collapse, in place.

For details, see full plan in **Brandschutzkonzept Ch. 6.2**, sent to Fire Safety Lead (Ole) on 2026-05-01.
