## Description

The Boiler Tent has been a cherished sound stage at Kiezburn for the last years. It is built around one simple idea: the dancefloor is the protagonist. The crowd faces each other — not the DJ. Behind the decks sits a living room, a sofa, a domestic intimacy at the heart of the party. Because music was never meant to be watched. It was always meant to bring people together.

This year we want to take it one level up or rather forward, backwards, sideways —instead of the camp-based Boiler Tent, we bring The Boilerwagen to the playa! Not a dancefloor, but little pockets of activity that can be easily packed up and moved around to surprising places. 


The Boilerwagen is a bizarre convoy of moving pieces:

* The core Boilerwagen: a pullwagon with the sound equipment that expands into a mobile DJ booth, built in a way to naturally break the "everyone faces the DJ" dancefloor dynamic and make the crowd dance around the DJ, facing each other.
* The Kunty sofa: a motorized (electric motor), drivable couch on wheels, together with a cozy vintage lamp, a foldable table, and a miniature club (sounds familiar? Remember KB2022?). 
* A small cart bringing deco, drinks and entertainment according to the day's theme and plans. 

  \
  When the Boiler Wagen & Kunty Konvoy are moving, everything is efficiently packed together - when it finds a spot to call home for the day, it expands into a cozy living room. Different themes and locations will give it a fresh energy every day. The visual theme is similar to the Pussy Wagon from Kill Bill: bright pinks and yellows.\n\nThis is not your usual sound art car: this is a commentary on our idea of what a "space" can be. What yesterday was a sad little dark corner in the forest is today a lush houseparty that makes you feel like you stumbled into a scene from a movie. It feels like it's always been there and it always will be - only to disappear with no trace a few hours later. The empty cemement in front of the effigy suddenly becomes a roller disco for a couple of hours. A fenced-off construction site pops up on the strip, with fake traffic signs, pink and yellow traffic lights, and convex mirrors, and that familar sound of bass.…or is it a Kuntstruction site? 

  \nUnused corners of Kiez Burn are suddenly not forgotten empty spaces, but an opportunity. We want "Where did this come from??" to be the sentence we hear the most this Kiez Burn.

## Justification of high value items

* Ecoflow Power Station: we need it to be able to have reliable power on-the-go. This is necessary for our mobile concept, since we want to be able to throw daytime parties that last more than a few hours, with good sound. If they are not funded by dreams, we will be forced to rent them. However, since we hope for this to be a returning dream, renting them every year would be missing on a good investment. Instead, we promise to keep bringing the dream back to Kiezburn and make this power station available to the community. Furthermore, this power model is manufactured for solar cell power storage. So for any power self-reliance purposes of the Kiezburn community as well as contributing to LNT it can also be used.
* E-bike motor (2x180): we want the couch to be fully motorized, like the cozy art car of your dreams. To be able to drive with 3 people sitting on it on uneven terrain, we calculated 2 e-bike motors. This is a necessary part of our dream and not something we can rent or buy used because of safety. They will be an integral part of our build: we plan to build them into the structure, which will lower their resale value significantly. Again, we plan to bring the dream back to Kiez Burn.
* Wagon (200): We looked at different options to buy the frame and the wheels separately. However, after speaking to our engineer on the team we came to the conclusion that buying a solid frame with wheels included is the safest option to prioritize stability and avoid the risk of it getting toppled over, since it will be very top-heavy. We need a specific model and size and we have not been able to find it second-hand. However, the wagon will not be the final piece: we will securely attach a platform to the top in order to create a stable surface for the DJ decks, and drill wood around it for aesthetic and functional reasons. This will lower its resale value.
* We realize the dream is a bit expensive but it is effectively a convoy made of two dreams: the driving sofa and the boilerwagen. We can split them into two different proposals if it's useful?


This is why we believe this is different from any moving structures we have had so far:\n1. it plans to be moving also in the forest. This is a very different terrain than on the strip! We are really serious about safety and stability to be able to achieve this.\n2. In my knowledge, the sofa would be the first motorized art car at kiezburn (without counting jungli, which only moved once for the effigy burn). I think that's really exciting! The ebike motor makes it safe and slow but also really inclusive - a lot more than pedal structures. We plan to offer rides on it to kiezburners, proioritizing people with mobility impairments for whom walking around too much (especially in the forest) can be a struggle :)
