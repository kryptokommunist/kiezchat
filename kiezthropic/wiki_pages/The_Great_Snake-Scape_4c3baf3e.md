## Description

***The Great Snake-Scape*** is a site-specific, immersive intervention that interrogates the boundary between primal terror and engineered leisure. By seeding exactly 100 hyper-synthetic ophiomorphic simulacra (rubber snakes) into a fragmented forest topography, the installation forces a state of radical visual panopticism. Wherever the spectator's gaze falls, the wild stares back. Not as a living organism, but as a mass-produced omen.

This inescapable reptilian matrix deliberately bleeds into the adjacent "Zooloon", an impromptu space of communal intoxication and kinetic catharsis. By juxtaposing the primordial threat of the serpent with the whiskey-soaked hedonism of the temporary dance floor, the work creates a spatial friction.

On a socio-political axis, the installation serves as a scathing critique of the Anthropocene's appetite for simulated danger. We live in an era where genuine ecological connection has been severed, replaced instead by the commodification of anxiety. The 100 rubber snakes represent the endless, manufactured micro-crises of the digital age – omnipresent, inescapable, yet fundamentally hollow. By staging this synthetic infestation within a space of forced celebration (the Zooloon), the piece exposes the contemporary human condition: dancing frantically on the precipice of an artificial apocalypse, fully aware that the threats surrounding us are fake, yet utterly paralyzed by the spectacle of them.
