## Description

My Dream is to server Burners with tea, and I'm planning to do so in two of the following ways:


1. **Afternoon Tea Ceremony** (semi-daily) – my biggest passion of the last years is the Chinese Tea Ceremony, and \[once again\] I wanna bring it to the Burn. It is an old tradition and art of brewing tea as a ritual, often done in silence as a mindful meditation practice. It's an hour long event, and I'm planning to do it multiple times, but probably not every day.\nIt is both a beautiful ceremony to ground yourself before an active day, as well as a journey into taste and smell. For me, this is a daily practice and I want to share it with the community. I have most of the stuff needed already, but it needs to be transported and the quality tea itself is costly.
2. **Samowar Pop Up** (semi-daily) – additionally to the above, I wanna bring this awesome samowar of mine and boil it on a couple of evenings, treating Burners with delicious teas (real and herbal) into their Burner cups to go. Similar to the Tea Room I realized at BurnHalla, but this time in the format of an occasional happening event, rather than a constantly running tea bar.

## Where at Kiezburn would you like your Dream/ Art to be?

**Nakti Nakti** is the place! We are going to have a wonderful dome for ceremonies, and we will put the Samowar outside, which is perfect given it's central location, as well as proximity to the saunas as well as the Effigy.

## Environmental Impact and LNT

It is very much LNT-compliant:


1. Tea Ceremony uses special teaware and the only waste are used organic tea leaves. And well, electricity to heat the water up.
2. Samowar is basically a big water boiler, so the only impact is the smoke from the kerosene lamp inside. And  the leftover tea leaves again. We will use metal tea strainers I purchased for the BurnHalla Tea Room, so no tea bags going to be wasted. Bio Müll collection will be taken care of.
