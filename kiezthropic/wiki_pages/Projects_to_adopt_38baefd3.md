### **The Map**

A 2\*3m painted map on-site to guide hippies into the maze. We all love a decent map full of useful information. Here's a collection of **__suggestions__** to make it happen! 

Structure:

* a Bauzaun and 2 blocks
* a few of these big plastic sheets
* approx 20 very thin zipties + duct tape

Setting up the sheets/boards requires 2 people:

* hold the board in place, draw 2 points at the back where you'll need small holes for the zipties to be attached to the fence. 
* get the person on the other side to pass the ziptie to the other hole. 
* when all boards are attached (3-4 zipties per board), reinforce the "weak connections" with tape. 

YOUR STRUCTURE IS READY!


What you'll need for the painting:

* a colour chart including: roads, forest, lawn, fences, camps (+ names), dreams/art.
* a few Molotow1forAll acrylic markers, because it's too much to write with a brush!
* a very light yellow spray for the first sketch
* a laminated map with the most up-to-date placement
* aceton in case you spray on the fence

Infos to include: camps, effigy, temple, gate, parking, free camping, 1st aid, feuerwehr, toilets, rangers, at least some dreams/art, fire space, no go zones, mailboxes if we have them, street names if you create them... 

Things that you __don't__ want to place / should remain a bit hidden: volunteers kitchen, site leads HQ, toolhouse, private areas when camps sleep further from their public part. 

Keeping the scale?

* it depends on the different levels of density: the strip was so full last year that I overrepresented it (and made the forest smaller)
* working with a grid is always an option… 

An interactive object? 

* some folks tend to add a sticker to the map: if you don't want that, it should be somewhat specified
* having a fun "map destroying workshop" on Friday evening with a handful of friends and sharpies can be a lot of fun! 

Potential difficulties:

* mostly the weather lead: it's impossible to work when the map is wet…
