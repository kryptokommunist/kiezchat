# ☀️ Solardome

# The Vision

The Solardome is the home for all makers, tinkerers and hackers at kiezburn. It is an incubator for smaller art projects, a makerspace with laser cutters, 3D printers and sewing machines, as well as the prefab-lab & storm-shelter for the effigy build crew. 

It is the nucleus of a solarpunk community, living by the values of a eco-sustainable but tech-friendly future. Where everyone is empowered to learn about their devices and be able to repair most of them themselves.

## Factual Description

The Solardome is a large steel 4V icosahedron half-truncated geodesic dome that we want to build from scratch for Kiezburn26.

### Sizing:

The Solardome is planned to be 4.9m high, 9.8m wide, 74.5m² ground area - designed to be just under the tent sizing limits from which [german bureaucracy](https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fliegender_Bau) starts inducing [headaches](https://www.profizelt24.de/partyzelte-pavillon/fliegende-bauten-rechtliches-beim-zeltaufbau/). It is the largest possible geodesic dome you can have in germany without requiring permits and the Amt to inspect it every single time you assemble it.

It is designed to be lightweight enough (<600kg) to fit on a small trailer, so anyone with a default car drivers licence (B) is legally allowed to pull the trailer.


### Materials / Sustainability / LNT:

The Materials are chosen to be long-lasting (aiming for 15+ years), cost-effective and low-maintenance. The Frame will consist of galvanized steel pipes and the shell of sailing cloth. The Solardome will be rust-proof, stable and storm-safe even in arduous situations ( for example Nowhere ) . 

We very much intend to bring this dome to every kiezburn for the foreseeable future. 


### Skills & Knowledge in the community:

We will do all possible parts of this build by ourself. We currently have a build crew of 15, of which 10+ members have a KB26 ticket, mostly coming from B.A.S.H. kiez.

We want to do all the build steps ourselves, like planning, designing, cutting, pressing, drilling, bending, sewing, testing….. everything…. because we want to build the skills in the community to repair and maintain this dome long-term. It is therefore also an investment into the skills in our community to build large metal projects. We also intend to release a self-help study guide on how to build a geodesic dome yourself.

We have found a metal workshop (Cadus) for manufacturing the steel struts, and a textile workshop (Haus der Materialisierung, Burner Embassy) for sewing the shell.

## Safety and Accessibility 

The safety calculations were advised by two structural engineers, to make sure the dome can handle mounted objects (solar!), suspended people inside (shibari or arial yoga or hammocks), as well as the most important one: wind load. The total surface area will be \~250m² which is no joke with high wind speeds.


## Building Experience

Metal building experience is quite scarce with this build crew, which is why we tried to connect via the burner scene to other, more experienced burners. They all joined the build crew after, so now we have, besides some very motivated home-DIY-Makerspace burners, also two structural engineers and two people with experience building previous metal domes for the nevada regional and nowhere. They will help us avoid costly mistakes and cut out process steps that are nice in theory, but a waste of money in practice (like post-process galvanizing and powder coating).

Most of the build crew is pretty new to large metal constructions though, and thats the point. We want to learn.


## Optional Upgrade: Future Thunderdome Playfights at Kiezburn

It is possible to plan this structure to have the calculated load capacity to enable future thunderdome-style playfights in the air. This needs to happen BEFORE the steel struts are ordered, as it is the diameter and wall thickness of steel struts that make this possible. A later upgrade is NOT AN OPTION.

The B.A.S.H. kiez members have overwhelmingly voted against hosting playfights at Kiezburn26, and therefore the base model of the dome is not designed to hold the additional load of playfighting burners. Still it is a consideration that needs to happen in the design stage, and can not be added on later. 

Structural calculations show, that adding the steel thickness needed would cost an additional 1000€ in steel struts, increase the weight of the dome from \~600kg to 1000kg, and therefore necessitate getting a larger trailer for transport, which in turn makes it necessary for all drivers to have a german BE licence to be allowed to pull large trailers - a quick ask around shows: very few burners have this. 

These practical and political restrictions make us hesitant to go for the more sturdy version of the dome.

UPDATE: the deadline for this upgrade has passed, we will proceed without it.


## Power usage estimate

The dome itself is not using any power, and is designed to mount on solar panels at a future expansion stage.
