# Sourcing material & LNT for artists

*This is a living document that should be completed as we go, feel free to comment or directly edit with your useful tips!* \n\nIt is commonly understood that LNT isn't enough, and that the limited resources of our planet should lead us to burning slightly differently than in the 90's. Here we started collecting a few useful links and tips to make your vision happen.  

## Be resourceful 


* set (an) alarm(s) on ebay kleinenanzeigen corresponding to your need(s)
* try the buy-gift-sell… category in the Berlin Burners TG
* check the online inventory of [Material Mafia](https://www.material-mafia.net/material/) and [Kunststoffe](https://kunst-stoffe-berlin.de/materialmarkt-im-hdm/)
* have a look at [NochMall](https://www.bsr.de/nochmall) (the BSR shop)
* check the [Kieztage](https://www.bsr.de/meinkieztag) next to your place
* try Free your stuff Berlin on FB

## A few art-specific LNT tips


General / random:

* if ALL zipties are reusable (sliding a blade to free the plastic bit), it is indeed easier to directly buy reusable zipties. DO IT!
* attaching things with tape is MOOPy: invest in a few ratchet straps or bungee cords to attach your pole tents at strike. 
*  

Using sprays

* spraypaint tends to flake on many surfaces (glass, tents…): test your surface before scaling up!
* the best background you can use (for all your on-site paint projects) is [Malerteppisch](https://www.bauhaus.info/abdeckungen-planen/abdeckvlies/p/20184832): paint makes cardboard unrecyclable, and painting right on the ground IS NOT an option! 
* to avoid the dark-grey water of your handwashing (if you don't use gloves), use aceton and an old piece of fabric to remove most of the spray from yours hands, and ideally spray/wipe your hands with water afterwards to avoid aceton ending in the ground.
