# 🤝 24/7 self-service station

# Description

The 24/7 Self-Service Station is a small but highly practical community resource at Kiez Burn that has been running for two years. It offers a freely accessible, low-threshold place where participants can find small everyday essentials whenever they need them, day or night, without having to ask anyone.

## Environmental Impact and LNT

The station encourages a culture of sharing and mindful use: participants are invited to take only what they need and, where appropriate, contribute useful items for others. At the end of the event, all remaining materials, storage elements, signage, and reusable items will be removed from site, reused, or responsibly disposed of.

The goal is to provide care and utility while keeping the station simple, low-waste, and fully aligned with Leave No Trace principles.

## Safety and Accessibility 

The 24/7 Self-Service Station is designed to be simple, visible, and easy to use. It will be set up inside a tent with one open side, making it easily accessible while still offering some protection from weather.

The items will be placed in three clearly organized boxes on a table, with each box easy to open from the front. This allows participants to quickly see and access what they need.

## Experience

The 24/7 Self-Service Station has been part of Kiez Burn for the past two years and has proven to be a simple but reliable resource for participants. It has been consistently used and appreciated, especially in those small moments when something essential is needed outside of regular camp interactions.

Through previous iterations, the setup has been refined to be practical, low-maintenance, and easy to understand, allowing it to function smoothly with minimal supervision. The experience shows that even a small, thoughtfully curated station can create meaningful support, encourage participation, and contribute to a sense of shared care within the community.
