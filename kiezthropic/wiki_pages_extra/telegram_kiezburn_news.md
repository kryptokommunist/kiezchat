**INCLUSION**
Kiez Burn welcomes people of all backgrounds, identities and experiences. As outlined in our Code of Conduct, we actively strive to be anti-racist, anti-fascist, anti-sexist, and anti-homo/bi/queer/transphobic. Every Kiez Burner is expected to extend equal respect and acceptance to all fellow participants.

If you witness or experience discriminatory behavior of any kind during the event, call it out! You can also ask the Rangers for help handling the situation. After the event, harm can be reported to the Kiez Burn Radical Accountability Division (RAD) using our [online Reporting Form](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSclIx7lsblpwtLDoJRPLrIIpvnE26Uk1ApSuKfKn_2LVRoddg/viewform).

**STAY VIGILANT**
Every single participant can help make Kiez Burn Safe*R. If you experience or witness something harmful, you can step in if you feel safe doing so, or find a Ranger to help. If you don’t see a Ranger around you can go to the Ranger HQ to call for help. Last year we had a few incidents involving material damage and offensive behavior, and together we can make sure these don't repeat. If something looks off, trust your gut: ask the person what they're doing, or ask to see their wristband.

💞 **Couples & Polycules @ Kiez Burn?**

Kiez Burn is all about radical self-reliance — and that includes your relationships. Attending as a duo or constellation can be magical — but also tricky. The Radical Accountability Division has crafted a Relationship Survival Guide full of tips & reflection questions to help you:

✨ Communicate clearly on-site
✨ Align on boundaries & expectations
✨ Be intentional with your time
✨️ Make space for solo *and* shared experiences

💦 **Keep it cool in the showers**💦

Keep the showes welcoming for everyone. Please respect each other’s privacy—don’t show up there staring or trying to socialise. Showers aren’t the place for sexy time with your partner; there is plenty of other dedicated spaces for that. Always ask before entering someone’s bubble, and leave the cubicle cleaner than you found it!

💬 **Consent**

We can all help making Kiez Burn safer!

Respect other people's bodies, personal space and time. Ask before acting! People have different boundaries and those boundaries are likely to change and shift over time. Don't expect people to like the same things as you do or want the same things that they did before. Make a habit of checking in with your own and other people's boundaries continuously.

Communicating your needs, wishes and boundaries can be difficult! It's a good practise to say "thank you" every time someone is able to express a boundary with you! This can make them feel safe and supported around you. Asking for what you want or suggesting an activity and receiving a no can bring a lot of feelings. Be kind to yourself and notice what comes up. It's not their responsibility to make you feel better about it afterwards. Remember that you are not automatically entitled to a reason either. Ask a friend for support if you feel like talking to someone about what happened. 

If you experience or witness something harmful during Kiez Burn you can contact the Rangers and seek support from the Welfare. But anyone can also be their own ranger! It’s ok to call out bad behaviour and help foster safer environment in each moment. 

The RAD has written a short guide on how to foster your own boundaries and support others: https://docs.google.com/document/d/1d7Dauc7uE6Xx6AV-H0vQusxNkJwVyV5vy6iPYmQF41k/edit?tab=t.0#heading=h.6xkwj1i08mc8

[2025-07-10] **PHOTOGRAPHY AT KIEZBURN**

We encourage you to give your mobile devices and cameras a break for Kiez Burn and embrace immediacy🔥 – staying in the moment, experiencing, participating🔥 and connecting instead of right away reaching out to your phone to take a photo.

If you decide to take a photo/video make sure to have consent🔥 from EVERYONE that appear in the frame BEFORE taking the picture / filming, even if they are far away. The same goes for the sharing of the photos. Ask consent🔥 from everyone in the photo before sharing it online – even in closed groups. Do not take photos in crowded areas like dance floors where you can't get the consent from all the people around you.

Many people like to be photographed but there is a large number of people who get uncomfortable when there is pictures taken around them and they can't tell if the camera is directed towards them. Let's gift🔥 everyone the freedom to radically self-express🔥 without the feeling of being captured on camera!



**PRONOUNS**

Considerable proportion of our community are LGBTQIA+ and we want to work together to make our events safer and more welcoming for our friends who are trans, genderqueer, or gender non-conforming. Here are some tips on how anyone can be an active ally!

🧡 Make it a habit to introduce yourself with your pronouns when meeting someone new. This can often make it safer and more comfortable for others who might not necessarily use 'traditional' pronouns to come out in that space.

💛 Avoid assuming someone's gender and/or pronouns based on your own judgement. It's always better to ask one more time if you are not sure! 

💚 If you make a mistake and notice that you are using wrong pronouns don't panic. Just simply correct yourself and move on!

💙 Respect people's privacy. Asking for someone's pronouns is ok, but asking them to tell you all about their gender identity is not ok. Educating yourself about gender diversity is important but don't make that anyone else's responsibility than your own.

💜 Pronouns can be new and a challenge for some people to learm at first. We all make mistakes, but the best way to show support as an ally is to keep practicing!

❤️  When we respect someones pronouns we affirm their gender identity. Your actions and allyship matter!
