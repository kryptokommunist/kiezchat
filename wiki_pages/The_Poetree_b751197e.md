## Description

Somewhere in a tranquil place in the secret forest, where the bass sounds only like a soft hum in the periphery, resides the poetree. It invites any roamer to contemplate, be still for a moment, write something, create, offering a possibility to turn soaked up inspiration into… art? You can recognize the poetree by its outfit: A small rug at its foot, on it placed a small tea table with writing and drawing utensils, some crafting material. A lamp and most importantly, the poetree book: An A4 blank notebook with good quality paper. Here and there poets will find a prompt in there like "What moves you right now? (in a haiku)", "The burner body hygiene song" or a thought provoking image. The branches will carry lanterns/fairy lights and maybe be decorated with some found objects from nature. It has rain protection - either a big umbrella suspended from the crown or a tarp.\n\nSo, what happens with all the poetree poetry? It's part of a continuous generative cycle (at least for the length of the burn, perhaps beyond it, who knows…), the fruit bears the seed for something new becoming:

* On Saturday or Sunday there will be a public reading of the poetree book either right at the tree or in a camp like e.g. the next stage. (Looking for readers!)
* Also on Saturday or Sunday, but after the reading, the poetry will be part of a dance improvisation workshop that works with spoken word from the poetree book and (hopefully) live music. We can imagine there to be a small performance at the end of the workshop. (Looking for workshop space and live musicians who like to improvise!)
