## Description

The Burnt Nest will be a space for people to experience authentic British Pub life. The word "pub" is short for "public house" – anyone is welcome at any time. 

We will have a bar, some comfortable chairs, colourful (disgusting) carpets, a darts board, a bell to ring and shout "last orders!!", and an old TV that plays old snooker tournaments – and perhaps if we're feeling spicy enough, a looped video of England beating Germany at the World Cup in 1966. \nWe will host classic British pub events: a pub quiz, a Sunday roast, and a "lock-in" – that's where a pub is open later than the official licensing hours and we keep the curtains closed so the (Un)Ordungsamt can't see inside to know that a party is under way.

There will also be an intense football rivalry with our two football teams (Kiezburn Rovers and their arch enemies Kiezburn Athletic Albion). We will get prop glasses that are safe to smash, and host a bar fight workshop – prompt cards will instruct participants on how to start a fight over football.  

Alcohol will be served, but it's low down the priority list. We plan to serve more milky teas than beers. There's also a great history of British music to be played from the pub radio.

This is a totally new project for 2026 so we have a lot of material things to sort out to realise the dream. We want to get as much as possible from Kleinandzeigen and flea markets. Almost all of what we buy will be re-used for future Kiez Burns. The plan is to develop the idea in future years with different gimmicks, and maybe a different theme.

## Where at Kiezburn would you like your Dream/ Art to be?

The Pub will be located in a Shelter/Tent that has the dimensions of 4,5m x 4,5m. 

Location will provisionally be next to the s-bahn train, on the strip.

## Existing funds

We will bring furniture we already own (chairs, kettle, kitchen stuff) and use some materials we already own (acrylic paint & gold leaf for the sign). 

As this is a new camp, we have a lot of costs associated with our own camp comfort and wellbeing, which will fund ourselves.

## Safety and Accessibility 

The structure will be safe as it is a standard coleman shelter, with lightweight decor hanging from that frame. The Darts board will be secured by a cordoned off corridor, so that no people passing by could accidentally be hit. 

Our structure should be quite accessible as it has large entryways and the furniture will be moveable to accommodate different needs. It will have comfortable seating. 

## Experience

We are a small new camp of 7 people who are going to co-produce this pub dream. We mostly know each other through our Burning Nest camp Hot Mess, which has produced a number of __[large and very popular pieces of fire art](https://www.instagram.com/hotmesscamp/)__. 

Two of our number have co-led the volunteer kitchen at Burning Nest for a number of years. A few of us have been to Burning Man and been involved in art projects there too, such as Illumination Village and __[the work of famed Burning Man artist Charlie Gadeken](https://www.charlesgadeken.com/)__. 

We all have EXTENSIVE experience drinking in British pubs. \n

## Power usage estimate

We will be serving Yorkshire tea and need to bring an electric kettle. Other than that it is mostly smaller items: a TV, some lights, and a living room sized speaker.
