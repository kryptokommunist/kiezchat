# Radio usage

To make a radio call, wait until the channel is clear and then **call their name followed by your name**. For example, if a care bear wants to reach the lead care bear say: "care bear lead for care bear, care bear lead for care bear." If you do not hear an acknowledgement the care bear lead did not hear you, or is not ready to take your call. Wait a moment and try again.

We need to know **3 things** to be able to mobilize resources effectively:


1. What do you need?
2. Where do you need it?
3. What do you need it for?


## **Example scenrio:** 

*There is no light and a puddle next to the toilets in the forrest. Care bears have been made aware and want to call the rangers to support.*

* Wait for the channel to be clear
* Press and hold button - wait ½ second - "**Ranger lead for walking care bear**" - release button
* Wait until you hear something like "site lead listening"
* Press and hold button again - wait ½ second - "**We need rangers to look at a power problem at the forrest toilet block**" - release button
* Wait for a response that lets you know that the issue has been understood and will be followed up upon


## **What to say and not to say**

**NEVER reveal sensitive information on the radio!** You don't know who might stand around a radio, only talk to the specific people on the designated emergency channel about specifics.

Generally try to be **as brief and precise as possible**, reduce your info to the essentials and necessities. Refrain from using radios for chit chat, others might need to use it right now.
