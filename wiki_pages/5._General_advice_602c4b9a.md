# General advice

## EMOTIONAL WELFARE

In addition to making sure you have all the necessary supplies to water, feed, and clothe yourself properly, please take care of your emotional needs. The Welfare team (location on the map) can help you if you are experiencing any emotional well-being issues.

● Listen to your body. Don't push yourself to the limit, and be more gentle with yourself than you would be in your regular environment.

● Be sure to stay hydrated and take time to rest.

● Sensitive to noise? It's a good idea to pitch your tent far away from the sound camps in one of the quieter areas. Bring earplugs!

● Intoxication has side effects. Bear in mind this can significantly affect your physical, emotional, and mental well-being both during and after.

The Rangers and Welfare are there to support you if you're feeling unwell.

## PERSONAL SAFETY

Kiez Burn is built on community, and can be a chance to make fantastic new friends – safely, one step at a time:

● Introduce yourself to the Wandering Rangers and volunteers (and volunteer yourself!).

● Let someone know if you are going to leave the site, and check in frequently.

● Use common sense when making new friends, and let someone know if you meet someone you feel unsafe with. Listen to your gut feeling.

● Make sure your valuables are in a safe place.

● Use your instincts.


:::warning
While there is a fence around the perimeter, local youth has sometimes scaled it to get onto the site, and things have been stolen in the past. 

**If you see someone climbing the fence, or you aren't sure if someone is part of Kiez Burn (that is, someone without a wristband), please notify the nearest ranger.**

If you cannot find a ranger, find anyone with a radio. Radios are always available at Ranger HQ and Gate. A security post is located next to gate, they will assist in matters relating to participant safety. Security are external contractors, always ask them to radio rangers if you interact with them.

:::

Kiez Burn can be a sexually charged environment, and there are many sexually explicit camps, workshops, and spaces at our burn. We want to help prevent unwanted incidents while ensuring a thriving environment for sex-positive freedom! It is imperative to know and express your boundaries - sexual or otherwise - along with asking about and respecting the boundaries of others.

## PHOTOGRAPHY

We are sure that you will want to capture some moments of Kiez Burn - but remember immediacy & consent when it comes to photography.

> **ALWAYS ASK PERMISSION. DO NOT TAKE SOMEONE'S PHOTO AGAINST THEIR WILL.**

In the event that you have a dispute with someone about photography, please flag down a Ranger so that the volunteers there can help mediate the situation.

Your right to take a photo and publish NEVER overrules other people's right to privacy! If you wish to post photos to Facebook or other social networking sites, MAKE 100% SURE that EVERYONE featured in your photos is happy for them to be posted. You should not be tagging people online unless you have their explicit permission.

> **NO photos should be taken and published for COMMERCIAL USE.**

We would like to keep a liberal photography policy providing the photographer respects photographic boundaries. Not everyone wants to have every moment captured and immortalized on film! Ask before taking any photos & videos! You must also confirm permission to use the photo/video as you so wish, e.g. publish on a blog or video-sharing platform. We are lucky to have very talented photographers in our community who provide us with photographic gifts for months following our burn. We ask you to practice immediacy during your burn but you have the choice and freedom to choose those moments to capture as you desire.

Please practice common sense and consent before being 'snap-happy'! No photographs or videos taken during the event may be used for commercial purposes.

Anyone recording videos must follow the same rules.

## RESPECT YOUR SURROUNDINGS

Please be respectful of the site as well as the people on it. Please pay attention to signage - if we ask you not to go somewhere/climb on something/light a fire there, it's for a reason. Everyone is responsible for their actions.

## FIRE RULES

● No Gas cookers in the Forest, except within the Huts (2x2m) that can be placed by us or the shipping-containers.

For Details refer to the cooking section. 

● No barbecues or fires that are closer than 50m to the forest and contained in a Fire bowl or ton. They need to be guarded by an adult at all times and a fire-extinguisher (6kg ABC and valid) must be placed close to it.

● No fireworks.

● Do not discard cigarette butts. Use a personal ashtray instead.

● No burning art installation, although we might be able to burn the effigy (Saturday) and temple (Sunday). Fire safety lead and KORG will decide that along with the local fire department.

● No playing with fire toys except in the fire space of the Prometheans Flames during the designated hours.

● Kids under the age of 14 years should be accompanied by an adult around active fires

> **Seriously folks, don't set the Gelände on fire.**

## KIEZE

The neighbourhoods of Berlin give it its character. So do the camps at Burns. Kieze (camps) are an integral part of the burn culture. They are the perfect way to come together with like-minded people to create and share together. Perhaps you would like to participate in a relaxation center with other yogis, or create a sound space for yourself and other music lovers? Kieze are organized collectives built around a theme or a common passion, enabling you and your fellow Kiez-mates to bring community and collaboration to the event on a larger scale, via your own autonomy.

Find out about starting your own here or how to get involved with existing Kieze by visiting the kieze section on our website. ==(Kiez registration deadline?)==

## FREE CAMPING

Some attendees might choose to Free Camp rather than join a Kiez. Free Camping can mean greater independence but also requires greater self-reliance compared to being a Kieze member. Free Campers must prepare and supply all their drinks, food, and shade, and dispose of their own rubbish. Free campers are still expected to participate through volunteer shifts, art, workshop, or performance projects. They do **not** need a vehicle permit.

## DOGS


:::warning
**Important: In 2025 there is a strict No Dog policy!**

:::

In 2022 we had a policy that allowed 5 registered dogs to accompany their humans. Unfortunately, not everyone followed this process and many unapproved dogs ended up being on site.  The owners of the registered dogs followed all guidelines but even then one interaction resulted in a safety incident, after which both owner and dog had to leave the premises.

Please **Do Not** **bring** your dog along to Kiezburn. This includes the Free Camping area or inside of caravans!

If you see a dog **Do Not approach** it and contact any Rangers. The owner and dog will be asked to leave immediately.

There are 2 dogs that live close to site. They usually stay away but might show up during Build or Strike. Again please do not approach them.

Please Note that officially registered and trained service animals can be excluded from this policy. As long as Gate and Rangers are informed and the animal is marked as such.
