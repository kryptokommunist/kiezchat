# Sound Rules at Kiezburn 2026

Questions about Sound? See also [Frequently asked Questions](https://wiki.kiezburn.org/doc/sound-rules-at-kiezburn-2025-3zphEJv3nc#h-frequently-asked-questions) at the bottom

# Information for all Participants

Kiezburn is not a music festival. While music is an important part of artistic expression, Kiezburn aims to strike a balance and allows room for all forms of Radical Self Expression, Gifting and Participation. This means having room for both all night partying as well as quiet meditative activities. 

As you can imagine this is a difficult task and requires communal effort from all participants. Let's try and create an amazing sound experience for all participants at Kiezburn.

At Kiezburn the ability to contribute musically is managed by creating three categories: Sound Camps, Non-Sound Camps and Freecampers. Each have different rules and possibilities.

## What changed since 2025

* "Regular" camps will be asked to specify their planned noise level and will be placed accordingly
* Sound Camp Rule changes:
  * Blue spots mandatory cardoid / bass directive setup
  * Blue spots playtime slightly extended
  * Firespace now blue
  * Dedicated mobile sound rules

# Rules for Freecampers

It's simple: Freecamping is a Quiet(er) zone. No speakers. 

# Rules for Camps

When it comes to sound, camps will exist in two categories: "Regular Camps" and "Sound Camps". \n\nThe 2026 Sound Camps are:

* Mighty Music Miners (red)
* Boiler Park (red)
* Cafe Wonderland (orange)
* Zooloon (orange)
* Firespace (blue)
* PsyKOtech Castle (blue)
* House of Plenty (blue)

All other camps are regular camps. Regular camps this year will be asked to sort themselves in noise level categories and will be placed accordingly. Sound camps can bring larger amplified sound systems, but are restricted in playtimes and will have their levels technically enforced by using limiters.

## Rules for Regular Camps

If you are not a designated sound camp, you will be asked during camp registration to put yourselves in one of three noise level categories. This will also affect the placement of your camp. Then, the following rules apply, depending on your noise level choice.\n\nThey tell you how loud your camp can be, and during what times. Outside of your sound times, no Sound can travel outside of your camp. So playing very quiet ambient music for example can still be okay. BUT: Any other participant can ask you to turn it off, and you must comply.

| Sound Level Category | How loud can you be at maximum? | What Equipment can you bring? | What are your sound times?  |
|----|----|----|----|
| No Sound | No speakers, no accoustic instruments, no noise besides people talking | Nothing | No sound at all times |
| Conversation | People are still able to hold a conversation without raising their voice. Background music is allowed | Small Bluetooth speakers (JBL Charge or similar), accoustic instruments | Between 10:00 and 22:00 |
| House Party | As if you were at a house party with the speakers cranked up. Music gets people to dance, but still your neighbours won't call the cops. You will have to raise your voice to talk.  | You can bring consumer grade gear like Soundboks, JBL Partybox, etc. - **no "serious" PA equipment, no Subwoofers!** | Between 10:00 and 15:00: Same as "Conversation" above<br><br>Between 15:00 and 22:00 (00:00 on Thu, Fri, Sat in the forest): House Party Level |


**In General**\nBe reasonable, talk to your neighbours. Find out what your neighbours plans and needs are, try to be respectful with each other.\n

### Sound Camp Placement

Soundcamp Placement will follow the same overall placement process as for other camps:

* In the camp registration form, indicate that you want to be a sound camp
* Give us the information requested
* Sound Lead / Placement Team will reach out to you

With the limited amount of sound camp spots that are possible, placement of sound camps is always difficult. As potential sound camps, please be open for solutions, think about collaborations, sharing spots and playtimes.


Your location colour will specify your playtimes and levels. Levels will be enforced with limiters.\n

 ![](/api/attachments.redirect?id=c7b91565-0d54-4b9e-b28e-342efb74934b " =1428x1002")

Quiet: Amplified sound system is off. 

75db: Turn off the subs, keep the levels low. If any of your neighbouring camps complain you will have to turn it down. These times are intended to play build background music for your camp, run some workshops, play some background music while serving chill drinks from the bar. They are not intended to run a full on dancefloor.

85db: Be considerate of your neighbours. Try to adjust your programming so other camps around you can still do activities that might require people to easily communicate.

We would encourage you to plan your scheduling and sound systems in a way that limits impact on neighbours:

* Cardoid Setups for Subs can reduce backwards emissions, which can be really helpful with a NW facing system on the site.
* Communicate with your neighbours during placement and try to adjust each others activities.

# Frequently asked Questions

### Do we have to get a Sound Camp spot if we want to play music?

No - you can still play music during 10:00-22:00 times, as long as you stick with the "[Rules for Non-Sound Camps](https://wiki.kiezburn.org/doc/sound-rules-at-kiezburn-2025-3zphEJv3nc#h-rules-for-non-sound-camps)". 

### Can we still play music after 22:00?

If you are a House Party camp placed in the forest, you can play music until 00:00. Otherwise, only if you are a sound camp. If you are not, no neighbours should hear any amplified noise from your camp.

### We are not a sound camp, can we bring a proper PA?

Unfortunately not! This is due to the premitting process with the Ordnungsamt where we have to specify excatly what our floors and expected sound emission levels are. \nThe maximum we can allow for non-sound camps is consumer gear like Soundboks, JBL Partybox or similar. No dedicated subs, no professional PA.
