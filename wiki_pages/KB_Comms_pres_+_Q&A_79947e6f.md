## How Did We Get Here?

We have a lot of different communication channels - 8 + a volunteer one and people find it very confusing.

Having so many principles and goals for our community has led to many different communication channels. We are quickly evolving in our digital infrastructure and communication methods.

The goal of the communication is to facilitate the flow of information between the people who have it and the people who need it.

The coordinicorns create and edit a comms release plan. The content is communicated by the channel realizers. For each communication channel there are different realizers that send the information through the platform. This is how the KB community members get information, but this is the start of the conversation. Moderators help with the discussions and forward feed back to the realizers, boardies, etc - all the sources of info and the coordinicorns.

Robots and comms have a lot to do with each other and work closely together. There can be some tense situations and communications that communicorns are managing.

## What are we doing now?

Which channel do we use for what info? It depends on who you want to reach

The Email newsletter is slower and it only has the most important updates.

Survival guide for ticket holders

## KISS

Keep it short and simple!

Use meaningful headlines to structure the message. Fonts, bolding, and emojis are your friend.

Highlight important info and/or repeat it.

Just because there's a comms post doesn't mean everyone will know or read it. It should be repeated if you want more people to know.

We're all volunteers and it's very possible that people may forget things.

#### **1. Communication Philosophy: The Organigram**

* **Goal:** Efficiently transfer information between those who have it (Realizers/Bordies) and those who need it (Community Members/Participants).
* **The "Coordini-corn":** A central role (or team) responsible for planning, writing, and coordinating the information flow across all channels.
* **Feedback Loop:** Communication is not just one-way. Moderators gather community feedback and discussion from Discord and forward it back to the decision-makers.

#### **2. Channel Strategy: Right Info, Right Place**

* **Public Face (Website/Tickets):** Entry point for new people; should not be flooded with logistical updates.
* **Information Backbone (Wiki):** The digital infrastructure where all data, history, and playbooks are stored permanently.
* **The Main Gist (Email Newsletter):** The most effective way to reach the entire community. Reserved for high-priority updates.
* **Fast-Paced News (Telegram):** Frequent, fast updates for active community members.
* **Discussion & Collaboration (Discord):** The primary tool for two-way communication, decision-making, and deep-dive discussions.
* **Annual Essential (Survival Guide):** The definitive document sent once a year to all participants.

#### **3. Message Design: The KISS Principle**

* **KISS:** "Keep It Short and Simple."
* **Visual Structure:** Use meaningful headlines, emojis, bullet points, and text formatting (bold/italics) to make messages scannable and memorable.
* **Repetition:** In an era of information overload, repeating critical information is necessary for it to "legitimize" and stick.
* **Volunteer Mindset:** Communication should be patient, recognizing that everyone involved is a volunteer with a life outside of Kiez Burn.

#### **4. Technical Challenges & Future Improvements**

* **Discord Rehaul:** Acknowledgment that the current Discord is "chaotic" and over-notifies users. Plans are in place to restructure it so users "land" in general areas and opt-in to specific channels.
* **Auto-Deletion Issues:** A known bug on Discord where past information is lost; the team is actively looking for a way to archive/stop auto-deletion.
* **AI Integration:** Interest in using AI for "Site Liaison" and Q&A (e.g., answering common participant questions based on the Wiki), though concerns remain about AI "hallucinations" (false info).
* **Wiki Maintenance:** A plea for community members to help keep the Wiki "clean" and updated with current playbooks and information.

#### **5. Open Roles & Action Items**

* **Critical Vacancy:** The "Coordini-corn" (Comms Lead) role is currently open.
* **Channel Leads:** Vacancies for Newsletter, Telegram, Wiki, and Survival Guide leads.
* **Discord Call:** A planned meeting with Fox to restructure the Discord server.
* **Wiki as Engagement Tool:** Encouraging free campers and new participants to co-create guides (like the "Free Camping Guide") on the Wiki.
* **Link Integration:** Suggestion to put a "Linktree" or similar easy-access portal at the end of the ticket checkout process so participants immediately find the right Comms channels.
