# ✅ **Volunteer Management Checklist for Realizers - long version**

*A simple guide to help you organize, support, and communicate with your volunteer crew — before, during, and after the burn.* *A mix of self‑check questions + practical **to dos***

## 🌱 **Before the Burn**

### **1. Shifts & Sign‑Up Page**

**Questions to ask yourself**

- [ ] Are all **shifts up to date** and accurate?
- [ ] Do volunteers **understand what they're signing up for**?
- [ ] Are the **critical shifts** staffed with a buffer in case of no‑shows?

**To dos**

- [ ] Add one clear, friendly **intro text** at the top of your shift signup list.
- [ ] Keep individual shift descriptions **short and easy to digest**.
- [ ] Add your **Build & Strike** shifts (each team handles their own this year).
- [ ] Double‑check the **confirmation email** volunteers receive after signing up.
- [ ] **Report changes** to the volunteer coordination crew on Discord.

### **2. Communication Channels**

**Questions to ask yourself**

- [ ] Do volunteers know **how to reach you** if they have questions?
- [ ] Does your team have **1–2 volunteer coordinators** handling questions, problems, and follow‑ups?

**To dos**

- [ ] Share your **personal Discord handle** or email.
- [ ] Make sure there's a **team-wide Discord channel** or a volunteer-specific channel.
- [ ] Clearly communicate **where volunteers should ask questions** (and who answers them).

### **3. Role Clarity**

**Questions to ask yourself**

- [ ] Is it obvious what the **role actually involves**?
- [ ] Is it clear if the role requires **physical work**, **emotional labor**, **technical skills**, or **training**?
- [ ] Are there any **learnings from last year** that can be implemented?

**To dos**

- [ ] Update **role descriptions** so expectations match reality.

### **4. Uncovered Shifts**

**Questions to ask yourself**

- [ ] What's our **plan** if some shifts stay empty?

**To dos**

- [ ] Check how you can **communicate your needs** to participants.
- [ ] Contact the **Communicorns** to add your open shifts to the newsletter.
- [ ] Post your needs in the **relevant Discord channels**.

### **5. Training (If Needed)**

**Questions to ask yourself**

- [ ] Do my volunteers need **training** before the burn?
- [ ] What topics absolutely need to be **covered**?
- [ ] Do I have a way to **track attendance**?
- [ ] What if people **don't attend** the training?
- [ ] Are the **training materials documented**?

**To dos**

- [ ] Offer one **online** and one **offline** training session if possible.
- [ ] Communicate **training times early and clearly**.

# 🎪 **During the Burn**

### **1. No‑Shows**

**Questions to ask yourself**

- [ ] What do we do if someone **doesn't show up**?

**To dos**

- [ ] Have a **backup plan** or a way to redistribute tasks.
- [ ] Avoid overloading the volunteers who **did** show up.

### **2. Orientation**

**Questions to ask yourself**

- [ ] Do volunteers know **where to go** and **when**?
- [ ] Do they know **who to check in with**?

**To dos**

- [ ] Share a simple **map** or pinned message with meeting points.
- [ ] Make sure volunteers know **how to reach you** if they get lost.
- [ ] Communicate with **Gate, Info, Volunteer Coordination**, and others who might be the first contact point for lost volunteers.

### **3. Shift Instructions**

**Questions to ask yourself**

- [ ] Do volunteers know **what to do** once their shift starts?

**To dos**

- [ ] Create a short **checklist for each shift** (tasks, materials, contacts, emergencies).
- [ ] Share it **before the burn** if shifts are complex, or **at the start of each shift**.

### **4. Documentation & Reporting**

*(for Rangers, Welfare, Safety, or any area dealing with incidents)*

**Questions to ask yourself**

- [ ] Do volunteers know **how to document incidents** before, during, and after the burn?
- [ ] Is there an **anonymous reporting option** available?

**To dos**

- [ ] Provide a clear **reporting process** (QR code, online form, logbook, etc.).
- [ ] Make sure reporting channels are **diverse**: online, offline, in person, anonymous, during and after the event.
- [ ] Decide **who receives and handles reports** and communicate this clearly in the report itself.

# 🌈 **After the Burn**

### **1. Feedback**

**Questions to ask yourself**

- [ ] Do volunteers have a way to **share feedback** on their experience?

**To dos**

- [ ] Collect feedback via a **form**, Discord thread, or a small debrief.

### **2. Documentation**

**Questions to ask yourself**

- [ ] Is everything important **documented** for next year's realizer?

**To dos**

- [ ] Write down what **went well**, what **didn't**, and what you'd change.
- [ ] Save all notes in your **handover on the wiki**.
