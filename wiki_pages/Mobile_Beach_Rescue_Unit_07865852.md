## Description

Somewhere between an art vehicle, a heat-delirium hallucination installation and a walking act/performance.

Built on top of an existing wheeled platform from last year, the Mobile Beach transforms corners of Kiezburn into spontaneous vacation zones. The platform will be covered by an oversized real-sand-haptic sandcastle, framed by inflatable objects (dolphins, flamingos) and beach items like umbrellas, palm trees and a treasure box. At the center is a tiny pool with a solar-powered cascade. At the back sits a raised lifeguard tower overlooking the chaos, searching for the spot where the Mobile Beach is needed most:

* Ocean breeze (4 mist diffusers with Mediterranean scent: essential oils of lavender, lemongrass, citronella and pine)
* Refreshment (3 Super Soaker 15L tanks to cool down the crowd or spray overheated Burners back to sanity, plus your favourite childhood ice cream: Beach, Sandwich, Flutschfinger, Kaktus, Nogger Shock, etc.)
* Beach vibes (sandcastle installation with lifeguard tower, mini pool cascade and inflatable dolphins)
* Beach care (choose between a sun lotion or tanning oil retreat; the platform also provides a shaded resting lounge with umbrellas and towels for exhausted participants during daytime heat)
* Beach games (our pirate treasure box is filled with frisbees, beach tennis gear and monster water balloons)

## Where at Kiezburn would you like your Dream/ Art to be?

The Mobile Beach can appear anywhere (mostly during the day when it's hot): the forest, the landing strip, the dance floor, or directly beside exhausted people melting in the sun.

Our over-motivated lifeguard squad is dragging the whole thing through the dust.

It is highly compatible with the BOILERWAGEN of our neighbours, The Cunts.

It is a must for any parade happening.

When traveling, everything can be secured tightly onto the platform. When parked, the structure expands into a fully immersive mini beach with lounging space, interaction and (water-)performing, lotion-spreading lifeguards.

We will avoid quiet zones and fully respect sound protocols and nighttime noise limitations.

The project works both as a daytime roaming interactive installation and as a nighttime glowing beach hallucination.

## Environmental Impact

A major part of this dream is based on reusing and upgrading an already existing mobile platform from last year instead of building a completely new structure.

The sandcastle structure will be made from upcycled paper.

Most decorative materials are reusable for future burns and events:

* umbrellas
* inflatables
* fabrics
* costumes
* lighting
* sound system
* water equipment

We will prioritize sourcing second-hand materials whenever possible (Kleinanzeigen, Flohmärkte, reused festival materials).

The project intentionally avoids loose "moopy" decoration. All inflatables and decorative objects are secured to the structure.

Water usage will be carefully managed:

* only clean water
* controlled spraying
* no soap or chemicals
* no creation of muddy areas

After every stop and at the end of every session, we will perform thorough MOOP sweeps.

The beach may disappear suddenly — but it will leave no trace behind.

### **Backpack Water Tanks + Super Soakers**

The interactive spraying aspect is one of the main participation elements. Instead of spectators watching the beach pass by, participants become part of the experience. We want the lifeguards to actively cool down overheated Burners and create spontaneous interactions across the festival grounds.

### **Large Decorative Props**

The oversized sandcastle sculpture is an eye-catcher.

### **Platform Reuse & Reinforcement**

## Safety and Accessibility 

* Structure moves slowly and manually
* Water spraying is controlled and consensual
* Lifeguard tower includes railings and stabilization
* Electrical systems are kept separate from water systems
* All decorative elements are secured against wind
* Multiple pull points allow controlled movement

Accessibility and interaction are core parts of the project: people are encouraged to join the convoy, interact with the lifeguards, rest on the beach, or help pull the structure through the playa.
