**The Grand Funeral**

The centerpiece of our Embassy's program: a fully planned funeral for a living burner.

Ever wondered about the day of your funeral? Who will come? What will they say to your still body? What would you want to happen? A funeral for a living person might be the most "in the moment" experience we can offer - you get to be present for a moment you'd normally miss entirely, and the people around you get to say the things they might otherwise save until it's too late. 

This is the once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to find out: Come and plan your own funeral with us, and we will make it happen. You will send out invitations to loved ones, make a playlist or decide on poetry, ask for a dress code... all before the ultimate day comes. You will meet your fate in the way you desire, be escorted by Death itself to our Embassy for final preparations before your guests arrive. A coffin will be involved. 

*Disclaimer: Nobody is dead, nobody is dying, nobody is being buried.*


**Further info about our Camp:**

The Embassy of Darkness is a newly forming camp and art project. We're a small crew looking to hitch a diplomatic post at Kiez Burn, representing a nation of the often-overlooked in such colorful lands: People of Darkness. 

KiezBurn is bright, loud, and wonderfully chaotic. But maybe it also needs a place for the other side? A shady corner where the drapes are dark, the music is 80s wave, and the sunscreen flows freely, because creatures of the night were not designed with daylight in mind. 

With our Embassy we want to foster a genuine cultural exchange between hippies and goths. This would be the first time for us to send out our ambassadors to wander the world of colours and our diplomatic mission carries a dual mandate: to *diffuse* gothic culture into Kiez Burn's colorful landscape, and to *diffuse* the age-old tension between hippies and goths. Like a drop of dark ink in bright water, we want to spread slowly, mix gently, and see what new shades emerge. And maybe it will turn out both sides like incense, make questionable fashion choices, and talk about the cosmos. We just disagree on the lighting.

And if the Embassy proves a success… well, maybe next year we will be more than just an embassy. 

**What the Embassy offers:**

* A dark, atmospheric space to sit and decompress: couches, drapes, druidic and witchy decorations, blankets, and one original, eerie 3D paper sculpture by one of our artists (see image above
* Workshops for dipping into gothic culture: crafting collars and necklaces, shirt and leggings weaving, "Meet a Goth," "Dance Like a Goth"
* Food offerings from the dark side (e.g. bloody pancakes, absinth)
* Free sunscreen, because we care about your skin

## Safety and Accessibility 

Our main structure is a pavilion providing a sheltered chill space. Workshops will be held in an open-air area adjacent to the camp, with the option to move under shelter in bad weather.

Lighting: we'll use solar and battery-powered fairy lights to mark our camp and help people navigate at night. In non-main areas we'll rely on participants carrying their own light sources.

The coffin is solidly built and sits on the ground or a sturdy surface. The structural load of a person lying down in a grounded box is minimal, but we'll make sure it's sturdy and free of splinters or sharp edges. 

Crafting workshops will be supervised at all times, and all tools will be collected when the workshop ends.
