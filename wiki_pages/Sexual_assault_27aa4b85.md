# Sexual assault

DEFINITION: 

A participant is forced into sexual acts (groping, harassment, abuse, rape) in which noconsent is given, potentially accompanied by actual or threatened (physical and/or psychological) force that induce fear, shame, or mental suffering. NOTE: NO RADIO COMMUNICATIONS about sexual assaults. None. At all. This is a very sensitive matter.



1. **CARE**: In cases of sexual assault the victim's care is paramount. They will be vulnerable, distraught, scared and embarrassed. The moment the sexual assault is reported they need to be brought somewhere private and safe where they can be cared for and law enforcement and medical can treat them. That should be the First Aid or Site Lead tent. 
2. **ISOLATE VICTIM**: Only Ranger Lead and/or Site Lead, and/or no more than one Ranger or Welfare volunteer specifically designated by Site Lead, depending on genders and qualifications, should be in a private location with the victim. The victim should not be left alone, not even for a second. The victim should be cared for by a member of the same gender, preferably someone who has had counseling experience.
3. **FRIEND**: In addition, the victim should be asked if there is anybody at the event that they would like brought to them and that person should be found.
4. **SUSPECTS**: Do not assume guilt. We are not a judge or jury. Treat the alleged suspect(s) with respect too. Leave legal matters and judgments to law enforcement! The alleged suspect(s) should be kept away from the victim. If the alleged suspect(s) is/are cooperative, they should be interviewed separately, out of earshot and view from the victim. As with Violent assault / Crime: No attempt to apprehend the alleged suspect(s) should be made and no one should put themselves in harm's way.
5. **POLICE**: Law enforcement must be called in if the victim wants. If the victim is not conscious or unable to make such a decision, the matter is automatically considered a serious criminal offense and law enforcement must be called. Full cooperation with local law enforcement at all times.
6. **PRIVACY**: Privacy of all parties (victims and suspects) is to be respected at all times, and the spread of rumours should be contained — all team members to actively assist in this. No sensitive information to be given via radios! - No names, no details!


## Immediate Actions - Sexual assault

 


1. Victims should be addressed and supported by same-gendered crew members, preferably with counseling experience, whenever possible. 
2. Sexual assault and rape are very emotional - please keep calm and professional
3. Do not assume guilt. We are neither judge nor jury. Please treat alleged suspects with respect too. 
4. If you are related or a friend of the victim or the alleged suspect you are not to have any contact with the case.
5. Ensure Victims privacy.
6. Do not attempt to apprehend anyone! 
7. If the alleged suspect(s) are intoxicated or agitated, they should not be approached. If they are out of control, Security or the Police should be contacted. 
8. Everyone will cooperate fully with the Police at all times. Only Site Lead and Ranger

   Lead will interact with the Police in a criminal case.
