## Description

Funky Holes is minigolf — with a twist. You put into human body parts.

The plan is to scatter the courses across all of Kiezburn, turning the whole event into a minigolf scavenger hunt, where each hole has a hint about where to find the next one. We'll coordinate with other camps to host individual holes at their spots. Every single course is absurd and creative in its own unhinged way: one moment you're catapulting a 1.80m beach ball three meters high through a hole. You blow a ball through a mini Barbie castle with a straw. Then it's a loop made of car tires in the form of a tongue and the final hole is a plaster cast of an actual ass. The goal is the asshole. Yes, you read that correct.

We'll be at Spark to investigate locations for our tracks and communicate with camps there. If our Dream gets funded, we'll bring at least 5 Funky Holes to Kiezburn.


## Where at Kiezburn would you like your Dream / Art to be?

Everywhere. That's kind of the whole point. The Funky Holes live across Kiezburn — individual courses placed in different Kiezes, plus anywhere we can claim a few square meters of unoccupied land. Some holes are tiny, for example a 20x20cm one tucked into a corner of Wonderland.

## Environmental Impact

We're building almost entirely from recycled and scrap materials. The body casts are papier-mâché with just a thin layer of cement to make them weatherproof. We're recycling old wood from our property in the countryside and collecting materials from trash piles and second-hand shops.

The courses are designed to be sturdy, weather-resistant, and reusable — the firm plan is to bring them back to Kiezburn year after year. Assholes and nose openings are made to last!

## Existing funds

We've gathered a solid pile of scrap materials: reclaimed wood, lots of fun found objects, second-hand shop treasures and papier-mâché. We're printing the game scoresheets ourselves.

## Safety and Accessibility 

The whole experience is extremely safe. It's literally accessible to everyone with hands and a head. No heavy machinery, no fire, no heights you can't walk away from. The courses range from ground-level putts to silly physical challenges, but nothing that requires more than basic motor skills and a willingness to aim at an anatomically correct plaster butt.

## Experience

We have plenty of experience with burns in general and have been active participants of the Promethean Flames for the last few burns. Two members of our crew study and work as professional artists, and one is currently tackling a similar sculptural project as her bachelor thesis. That said — at the core, we are confident dilettantes, and we mean that as the highest compliment to ourselves.

## Power usage estimate

To make the courses fun at night, we'll have a handful of black-light LEDs that are battery-powered and charged at our camp using camp energy. For one of the courses, we're planning to use a bigger black-light-LED that is powered with a cable drum. As a bonus efficiency move, we'll repurpose existing film batteries to power specific lights.
