# Dreams Funding Rules

# Dreams Funding decision-making process

### What we do and don't fund, and why

## Last updated: January 2026

We want to encourage creativity and freedom of thought and expression from the dreamers applying and are not proposing that an application needs to cover all of the listed criteria to be successful, these are just a way for you and us to assess if a proposal will work, and what it will bring to Kiezburn. To absorb some of the subjectivity of the criteria we have a committee to discuss each application; the applications will be scored against these criteria by this committee. If you would like to join the committee, please get in touch. Committee members will never discuss or score an application they have submitted, nor have another conflict of interest.

## Decision making criteria:

### General Plan: 

* Has the development of the project been thought out thoroughly and described clearly?
* Is there a realistic build plan/timeline or has it been built already?
* Is it clear what the project is about and what it offers to Kiezburn?
* How does the proposed Art fit with the vision of Kiezburn and is it in tune with the Principles?

### Deadline:

The deadline for Dream Applications is **22nd of May 2026**

### LNT Plan: 

* Is there a clear plan for assuring the project and any space it uses will be left with no trace or a better trace?
* Has there been consideration for avoiding materials that will likely produce MOOP or become easily unattached in bad weather?
* Has there been consideration for using materials that won't pollute the land or contain toxic elements?
* Has consideration been made to source materials second hand or recycled?

### Health and Safety:

* Is the plan well thought through to avoid safety and health hazards?
* Will all structures be built so that they are safe for participants to interact with, and can withstand the weather? Are the materials used non-toxic to the land and participants?
* Will any outdoor structures be well-lit at night?

### Budget: 

* Is the proposed budget detailed and realistic?
* Are they asking for anything irrelevant or listed in the "things we don't fund"?
* How much of the project uses second hand/reusable items?

### Vision

* Is the project *awe-inspiring or ridiculously fun?*
* Does the project create interaction with fellow burners or can it be considered *radically participatory?*
* *Does the project elicit personal reflection*? Is it *thought-provoking?*

  *We want to support the formation of camps, as we think they are a core element of what makes Kiezburn unique as a community-driven Burn.*

  *We want projects which you will remember and tell all your friends about. We also want projects that are only possible in the unique space that is a burn, and are therefore really hard to explain to your non-burner friends.*

## Things we don't fund:

* Tools: Artists must bring their own tools needed to build and strike their projects, and for any maintenance they may need during the week. 
* Consumables like Food, Alcohol, other intoxicants, Gifts
* Payment for craftsmen's, or artists', or performers' time
* Camp infrastructure, such as generators, domes, pavilions, pre-built durable items intended to build up a camp's space (we recommend this come from camp fees and your own fundraising as a camp)
* Personal services, e.g. personal training/classes, child care, carpentry, fee-for-time services
* Rental of equipment paid directly to the requester of the refund
* Buying finished products/services from your own company
* Single-user licensed software used to create a project
* Any thing that is not obviously and directly related to creating art that will be displayed at our events or our event production
* Anything illegal or unsafe
* Things ordered from TEMU, aliexpress are complicated to refund, so consider somewhere else if still cheap!

### Other considerations:

All art that includes fire, high-powered lasers, or significant heat must get approval of the fire lead, and if approved, have adequate fire suppression materials on hand at all times and be attended by a sober adult at all times that flames are present.

If you apply for a grant for an item that has high resale value (like tents, speakers or electronic devices) Kiezburn reserves the right to add this item to its Dreams Inventory after the Burn - so it can be stored and reused for future Kiezburns.

Transport costs for the projects are covered, but only covered for the fraction of the cost that the actual project takes up!
