## Description

A Schlauchboot with Wheels, dressed up in pretty clothes and filled with cozy pillows, blankets and softness. An attached rope lets it be pulled & relocated. An attached tarp can be deployed to fully cover the boat when it stars to rain so everything inside stays dry.

* **Need a little break but FOMO is haunting you? → Snuggle up and have you friends pull you along**
* **Field-Deployable solution for spontaneous cuddle sessions**
* **Perfect hiding place**
* **Holds up to 6 People**

**Dimensions**

Depending on the boat, but aprox.

* Inner-Area (where you can sit, lay down): 3.3m x 1.5m
* Total Size: 3.6m x 1.7m

## Environmental Impact

All of the Materials are Reuseable: Be that for bringing this same Dream to future Burns, or re-use for other Dreams, nothing will go to waste.

I will try to source as many things as possible second hand from kleinanzeigen or similar

**Boat**

The boat is a hunk of plastic, that can become damaged and unusable. To mitigate this, I for one have the tools and know how to fix any holes it might get. 

I am also looking into fixing up a used one, if I can find one that is still in a good condition and reasonably priced. 

Consideration also went into the fact how to not have it get holes in the first place. All sensitive parts will be protected by various fabrics and other soft things:

* Pillows / Foam mats + thick felt like plastic fabric at the interface of boat & undercarriage
* No exposed boat skin at all
* Pool noodle bumpers on the outside
* Matraces, Pillows and fabric on the inside

## Safety and Accessibility 

* Wheels are Rated for 920kg in Total
* All Protruding parts are soft (Inflatable Boat, Pool Noodles, Fabric) making potential accidental collisions soft and cozy

**Breaks**

The current plan does not feature any occupant trigger-able breaks. The reasoning behind why none are needed: 

* The site is very flat, so no hills to roll down uncontrollably (\~1-2m height variation along the entire Landing strip)
* The only way this thing moves, is an outside person pulling the boat along
* The Tiny swivel wheels don't keep up speed for long when not under thrust 
* Real boats also don't have breaks

If there are any concerns, this could be added
