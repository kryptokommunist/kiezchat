# Practical Guidelines for Supporting Affected People

This is a general guideline which may not be suitable for more urgent situations.


\
* Meet the person at eye level
* Introduce yourself with name and role ("Hi, I'm X from the Welfare team. We're here to make sure everyone is doing as well as possible...")
* Create a calm atmosphere / open body language (e.g. don't cross arms, open palms)
* Validate and affirm the affected person in their concern
* If needed, briefly explain what Welfare does
* Confidentiality of the conversation
* Communicate the time frame, adapt to the situation ("We can talk calmly for about half an hour now.")
* Offer to leave the situation / ensure a safe environment (accompany to the Safer Space if needed) / clear physical distance from the person who caused harm
* Look for resources for a positive way forward ("What would feel good for you right now?")
* Try to break thought loops ("What can we concretely do right now to make the situation more comfortable for you?")
* Bring in a trusted person if needed
* If the affected person comes with company, agree that only one friend stays, arrange a pick-up time if needed, make sure the affected person actually knows and trusts this person (take them aside and ask directly if unsure)
* End the conversation, summarize what was discussed, affirm, offer information materials, emphasize resources. E.g.: "Our time is slowly coming to an end. We've discussed various options. Take a moment, and feel free to come back to us anytime.")
* Obtain consent
* Refer to further support resources if needed (RAD report, leaflets in harm reduction)
