## Description

Let me take you on a journey through time and space… and through more time…. then back again.

It's a big blue(ish) box that's even bigger on the inside! A truly original idea!


The Dimension Doors is an existing yet incomplete piece first made in 2021. It was recently updated to a new design for the ELS, but some noticeable bugs and minor hardware issues mean I want to upgrade it to its truly magnificent level.


The box contains experiences, synchronized light and sound, and selected via a Keypad. It utilizes the infinity mirror effect along with addressable LEDs to guide users on complex metaphysical journeys, ensuring they leave the box in a different timeline than they went in (so long as they participate and don't spectate).


The Dimension Doors as a structure has survive Melt festival (20k guests, mostly just drunk), and so has prove itself event-ready.

What I want to do now is the following, in order of priority:


1. Fully light-seal the box. Currently it's 99% sealed
2. Improve the electronics to make it more reliable and easier to update on the fly.
3.  Add mirrored acrylic to the ceiling and the floor, further enhancing the infinity effect (dramatically)
4. Install a Sub Transducer so that the sub-frequencies are being created by the resonance of the entire box, as opposed to an adjacent subwoofer. 
5. (max level ambition) - Train an OpenClaw agent to take voicenotes via Telegram and create completely custom experiences.

## Safety and Accessibility 

The Structure it rather massive, and stabilized on a heavy duty pallet. The walls are made of acrylic and wood, so will not survive a human throwing themselves at it with full force, or inconsiderate fucking, but falling on them or leaning on them is not an issue.

The electronics work at maximum 12V, and so are very safe even in the worst case scenario. All components are contained in a waterproof housing.

The Main risk is with the door, which if there is no external structure to support it, could swing open in strong wind. I will set up the box to ensure that cannot happen (which is easily done).
