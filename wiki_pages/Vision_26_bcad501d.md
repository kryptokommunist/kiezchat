# Vision 26

This document is the result of a consultation with former dream/art guides and leads, discussions with artists and realizers but also a thorough study of the feedback given in the census. It also follows the RAD's recommendations to the KB26 art team. We hope it will inspire individual and teams to amaze us this year! 

# Goals & Challenges

	Following the intention formulated in the __[precedent AP](https://wiki.kiezburn.org/doc/ap-dreams-art-grants-gEd8rpmGaj)__, such as the __[selection and refund criteria](https://wiki.kiezburn.org/doc/dreams-funding-rules-pgVOvKrQ3s)__ suggested by the former art lead/team, we wish to dig deeper into how and why we do what we do. The art budget represents about a third of our event budget, and is supported by every ticket holder. This comes with a set of responsibilities to curate the "best" possible experience for KB participants. 

	A few questions guide our reflection:

* how to minimise the curating power of the art team? 
* how to make sure art doesn't end up in a board/treasurer nightmare? 
* how to build trust with our artists?
* how to integrate new artists in our community?
* how to build trust within the community? 
* how to facilitate art that will leave traces in people's minds?

# Our values and method 

## Accountability

The art team and artists have responsibilities towards each other. From both sides, we can only expect the respect of our principles and code of conduct. From the art team side, it means:

* acknowledging our biases: and not only when it's about our friends! Some type/genre of art may not speak to us, and we wish to cultivate an open communication about these topics, encouraging team members to take a step back when they feel "too biased". 
* We commit to a clear and understandable process. Our criteria, timelines, and decisions are shared openly whenever possible, so the community can see how and why choices are made. 
* accepting extra control over the team's own art projects: from the project description to its delivery and reimbursement process, our standards for ourselves should be exemplary. 
* Feedback from the community is essential. We welcome input, questions, and reflections both before and after funding rounds, to help us grow and stay accountable to our shared values.

From the artists' side, it means:

* supporting our work by being kind, respectful of our time and boundaries. 
* carefully reading the funding guidelines and sharing them with any member of the team. Difficulties with receipts lead to a much longer reimbursement process for everyone, which we cannot afford. Our treasurer is also a volunteer, please respect their time!
* delivering the piece in due time (for instance, max 36 hours after the start of the event)

  \

##  Diversity 

Being more resourceful will support diverse artistic expressions by allowing more projects to take place. Here is what we mean by diversity:

* in scale: big, small - what is average? We wish to offer a multi-scaled experience, because we are not in a museum or a gallery where coherence matters. We want contrasts - being amazed by the big, and getting the thrill to find the tiny. 
* in style/genre & type of art/projects: when we think about "Burner art", we all have different images in mind. Interactive, inspiring, made of lights, pigments, sounds or fabrics, fire or moving bodies; nurturing connection or self-reflection, or just fun and silly. We'll also keep an eye on enabling various projects and avoid redundancies. 
* in demographic representation: Everyone is invited. We actively celebrate diversity of gender, age, race, sexuality, ability, culture, and perspective. We strive to ensure that our funding decisions reflect the wide range of people who make this community what it is. We want to enable newcomers as well as trusted seasoned Burners. 
* Inclusion and ethics are not fixed goals but evolving practices. We commit to learning, listening, and adjusting our approach each year to better serve inclusion and participation. 
* We finally wish to raise awareness about cultural appropriation by providing guidelines and orientation to our artists, while addressing the art team's privileges. 

## Resourcefulness

Resourcefulness means - for the artists:

\- [using all possible free or 2nd-hand options](/doc/sourcing-material-lnt-for-artists-Wk34OwMY3C) before considering buying something new: it is less about money than about the scarcity of resources of our world, and in solidarity to other artists to bring their contribution.

\- building a cooperating network of KB artists regarding (storage/working) spaces, tools, materials or skills. 

\- remaining flexible, planning ahead


For the team:

* Updating and actually using the material list from previous years. 
* (Ideal world scenario) Developing a material library for artists to use. 
* creating a space for KB artists to be in touch
* investing time into checking the material lists and discussing with teams what could be easily sourced
* as a consequence, the team should refuse fewer projects and therefore curate less: our goal is not to be *kunst kunts*, but to enable as many projects as possible to take place.
