\n**Can I take pictures?**

[Jein!](https://www.urbandictionary.com/define.php?term=jein)

Consent is our 11th principle. We would like to keep a liberal photography policy providing the **photographer respects photographic boundaries**. Respect that not everyone wants to have every moment captured and immortalised on film! Ask before taking any photos & video! 

You must also confirm permission of everyone in the photo/video to use it as you so wish, e.g. publish on a blog or video-sharing platform. 

We are lucky to have very talented photographers in our community who provide us with photographic gifts for months following our burn. 

We ask you to practice immediacy during your burn but you have the choice and freedom to choose those moments to capture as you desire.

Please practice common sense and consent before being 'snap-happy'! No photographs or videos taken during the event may be used for commercial purposes. 

Some notes on this: 

* Asking for consent of everyone in the picture means you cannot take pictures of crowds! 
* 360° videos "of the vibe around" is absolutely not ok!
* Respect the principle of immediacy also of your fellow burners! Standing in the middle of the dance floor with your phone up high, even if you are just using Shazam, will make people around you uncomfortable. 
* **Please, remember that Kiez Burn strives to be a safer\* space, where we hope to be able to feel free enough to do what we want. Having phones around pointing in different directions inhibits and restricts this freedom**.

**Can we have a gas cooker?**

Yes, you can! But, Achtung! 

* The gas cooker **needs to be on a stable self-standing structure**. 
* A valid **fire extinguisher needs to stand next to it, accessible and visible.** Please check that is still valid (usually 2 years)

Think like: having an open flame so sturdy and stable, that a "confused" passing by hippy won't burn the whole Brandenburg 👌

A lot of the more simple versions are shaped like a tower, together with a pot of food, this will be too wobbly and likely to tip over. Those won´t be allowed. It needs to be on a normal height table (to have it far from the ground) that has a stable standing. 

## \n**🔥 Fire safety**

**What are the fire requirements for the Kieze?**

Each Kiez will have to bring their own valid 6kg fire extinguisher, visible, accessible and not expired (check the expiry date, they require checking every 2 years). There will be someone checking on site. If you do not have a fire extinguisher, you will need to purchase one. They can be found at the Baumarkt for \~30 euros.

Keep the fire extinguisher visible, and especially close to any dangerous areas (open, electrical equipment etc). A random guest in your camp would need to be able to find the extinguisher if they need it, your deco-theme is secondary to this.


**How about cooking?**

* No Gas cookers in the Forest, except within the Huts (2x2m) that can be placed by us or the shipping-containers.
* Gas cookers outside the forest can only be operated when placed on a table (min 50 cm high, stable) and next to a fire-extinguisher (6kg ABC and valid) and a fire-blanket (to extinguish humans and oil). This applies for camps and freecamping.

\n**Can we have a firepit or a barbeque?**

That will be up to the fire regulations at the time of the event. 

* No barbecues or fires that are closer than 50m to the forest and contained in a Fire bowl or ton. They need to be guarded by an adult at all times and a fire-extinguisher (6kg ABC and valid) must be placed close to it.

**__[Check the Waldbrandgefahrenstufe](https://mleuv.brandenburg.de/mleuv/de/umwelt/forst/waldschutz/waldbrandgefahr-in-brandenburg/waldbrandgefahrenstufen/?x=1752738137)[.](https://mluk.brandenburg.de/mluk/de/umwelt/forst/waldschutz/waldbrandgefahr-in-brandenburg/waldbrandgefahrenstufen/#)__** The new location is close to Jüterbog in north-eastern Germany, in the Teltow-Fläming district of Brandenburg.


**Smoking**

Do not discard cigarette butts. Use a personal ashtray instead.


**Can we burn stuff at the end of the event?**

That will be up to the fire regulations at the time of the event, and can change on a daily basis. Make sure to check the current fire status with the Rangers on site before doing anything dangerous to nature in the area. We are not in a barren desert.

## **💧 Water** 

**Will we have water at Kiez Burn?**

Yes.

**So we don't have to bring our own water?**

No.

**How do I get water from the tap to my camp?**

It looks like this year water will flow. There will be water faucets on site. It will be the responsibility of people in your camp to pick up the water from the faucets and take it to your camp. Maybe consider a pulley as water could be heavy in those containers. You can also bring hoses, T-pieces (a 3 way connector) and whatever you want. We recommend coordinating with the neighbours that you are sharing the water source with to find the best solution. 

You can contact the Water Lead in Discord if you have questions. (You don't know who that is? Check the wiki page "Leads and Responsibilities)

—> The faucets at site are **12.7 mm (1/2 inch)** so the normal garden hose size. 

—> Kiez Burn sometimes also distributes water with a high-pressure 20mm hose, in case you want to think of connecting to those. 

\n**What can we do with grey water from the camp?**

==At this point, we believe we can empty the grey water from camps on site but there will be a special place to do that. Please check back for more info on that specific place.==


**Will we have a restricted amount of water we can use?**

Not at the moment. Water costs 1.60 per cubic meter, so it's cheap. If you have a dream that uses a lot of water, please reach out to water team. Otherwise, water is paid for by Kiez Burn. Meaning camps will not have to pay extra costs for water consumption. Brandenburg is one of the driest areas in the whole of Germany and is already struggling with water shortages – just be mindful when using this natural resource.


**We can have a pool?**

Yes, you can have a pool. In fact, we are encouraging it. 


1. During hot summer days, we have to keep cool somehow, so why not a pool?
2. We are helping the fire department with water distribution across the site in case of a fire.

If your camp wishes to get their own pool or you want to share a pool with a few camps it will help a lot. Please coordinate with the water lead to have it filled once on site. The only wish is to keep the water natural, so no chlorine tabs. To keep it clean, get a pump with a filter.

**BUT PLEASE, know there is a responsibility you hold in your Kiez if you have a pool. Make sure it is being watched at all times, especially if you are in a quiet Kiez, at night or early hours of the morning.**

## \n**💩 Shit Ninjas** 

**Do we need to allocate shit ninjas?**

Yes, each camp will have to have 2-3 dedicated shit ninjas. There will be training on site for the shit ninjas so these allocated people will need to be there.


**What do shit ninjas do?**

We need 2 people from each camp to help mind and manage the toilets on site. ==Basically it's about changing the barrels out and making sure that toilet paper and saw dust is in the toilets near your camp.== There is training for the shit ninjas on the Wednesday. The job sounds unpleasant but it's really not and super easy. You keep an eye on the toilet and if it needs changing you get another shit ninja to help and you change out the toilet. We are a radical self reliance festival and there are no classically functioning toilets on site. This is a vital part of the functioning of the playa and each Kiez needs to allocate 1-2 people to manage the bathroom in their space. You can expect to maybe do this once or maybe twice per shit ninja for the whole event. Takes 10mins each time.

## **🛠️ LNT**

**LNT is much more than just leaving our Kiez clean (which is of course needed and important).**

LNT is about how we can reduce the ecological footprint of our Kiez and of Kiez Burn in general!

\n**What is LNT protocol?**

We would expect you and your camp to have an LNT protocol, that has more or less this information: 

* a plan to reduce waste before the event
  * plan accordingly, reduce package, pre-cook, re-use, re-cycle
* a plan to organize waste during the event
  * LNT station, enough rubbish bags/boxes/cubes/containers, a place to gather the full bags, appropiate containers to transport glass or [Pfand bottles](https://www.how-to-germany.com/pfand-in-germany/) (gathering glass containers in a plastic bag or a cardboard box was always a very poor idea.. The plastic bags brake and became a hazzard, the cardboard gets wet and basically disappears)
  * Protect the trash! Fly away rubbish will end up in the fields nearby, and we don't want that.
* a plan to get rid of the waste after the event
  * who is taking what where? how? 
  * Plan a Strike MOOP sweep! Nothing must be left on your area after you're gone.

    \

**Some other notes on LNT:** 

* There will not be a place to dump your stuff and no one is expected to clean up after you. There will be an inspection of your Kiez site after the event, before we hand the site over. You've been warned.
* Correct: there might be a place to get rid of your BIO-WASTE on site, paying a small amount. Check on Discord how to get access to this.
* Think of reducing your Kiez eco-footprint while planing your Kiez. Reuse over rebuy, check where you make your purchases.
* Soap and shampoo should be bio degradable. 
* NO GLITTER - NO FEATHERS - PUNKT
  * oh.., really? But what about bio-glitter? Aren't feathers just part of nature? We've been having this discussion basically every year since Kiez Burn started. Bio-glitter just takes less time to degrade than the normal and impossible to tell apart. If we find glitter on the floor, we'll never know if it's biodegradable and will have to clean it anyway. Not fun. Feathers fly away easily and  end up everywhere. Unless you have the feather of a bird leaving in the area, you'll be technically introducing exotic bio-material.

## \n**🔊 Sound**

**Do I have sound limitations?**

Yes, there are sound limitations. There are only a few camps - in a dedicated zone of the site - who are allowed to play loud music and bring a sound system. You will need to register as a sound camp if you wish for your camp to be a sound camp.  

All Kieze that are not part of this dedicated area are not allowed to bring a sound system. In case you bring one, the rangers will ask you to unplug it.

All Kieze are allowed to bring a small bluetooth speaker. 

\n**Can I play music during the build?** 

No. For the build, we will require a "silent build". There will be no speakers and sound will not start until the beginning of the event.

\n**Can I make a sound camp?**

Yes. All sound camps need to be registered as sound camps due to local authorities regulations. That includes all projects that require a sound system larger than a bluetooth box (which means that the equipment will need power from generators). If you wish to participate in sound at Kiez Burn, you MUST apply for it.

Please note that the number and placement of sound camps is also heavily regulated by the authorities and the site itself, and we just need to comply to it if we want to burn there.
