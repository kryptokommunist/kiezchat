# Leadership following the principles

## Key takeaways (based on session audio recording) 

#### **1. Redefining Leadership**

* **Leadership as Facilitation:** True leadership in the Kiez Burn context is about creating space for others to become "little leaders" rather than top-down management.
* **Adaptive Leadership:** Leaders must be "organic" and experimental, knowing when to step in to provide structure (especially in chaotic environments) and when to step out to allow the community to self-organize.
* **Agency and Empowerment:** A primary goal of leadership is ensuring every participant feels their agency and sees the direct outcome of their contributions.

#### **2. The "Trash Fence" (Personal Boundaries)**

* **Resource Management:** Leaders must identify their own "Trash Fence"—the point where their time, energy, and social battery are exhausted.
* **Preventing Burnout:** some folks mentioned the "Mommy trap" where a leader ends up doing all the work because they fail to set boundaries. The group agreed that sometimes you have to "let the gap exist" rather than filling it yourself to encourage others to step up.
* **Passionate Leadership:** A strong boundary is only taking on tasks that "excite" you. If a leader isn't passionate, they should enable others but not own the task.

#### **3. Community Boundaries and Inclusion**

* **The Paradox of Tolerance:** While Radical Inclusion is a principle, the community must have a "Trash Fence" for behavior that harms the community (e.g., hate speech, ignoring consent, or failing to respect others' boundaries).
* **Clashing Principles:** Leaders often have to navigate conflicts between principles, such as Radical Self-Expression versus Civic Responsibility. There is no "right" answer; it requires a situational decision by leadership.

#### **4. Teaching the 11 Principles**

* **Acculturation over Filtering:** The focus should be on "teaching by living" rather than just filtering out those who don't know the rules.
* **Beyond the Link:** Sending a URL to the principles is ineffective. "Hippies can't/won't read." Principles could be taught through:
  * **Rituals:** Incorporating principles into every meeting or gathering.
  * **Acculturation Calls:** Short, informal meetings for newbies to explain camp culture and expectations.
  * **Ambassadors:** Long-term community members who act as mentors and value-teachers.
* **Language and Buzzwords:** Using principles as "buzzwords" in daily tasks (e.g., thanking someone for "Communal Effort" during a kitchen shift) makes abstract concepts graspable.

#### **5. Intentionality**

* **Building Community, Not Just Logistics:** Leadership isn't just about the truck arriving on time or the infrastructure being built; it's about the *vibe* and the social fabric of the event.
* **The "Why" Behind the "What":** Leaders should always communicate *why* the principles exist to help newcomers understand the purpose of the Burner community.
