# 1. Preparing food

### FOOD & DRINK

You must bring all your food and drinks for the event, storage for your supplies, and any cooking equipment you require. There will be no food or drinks sold on site. There is access to water at several stations around the site, but if you choose to drink anything besides tap water, you must also bring this with you.

● You will be able to use a cooking unit that requires electricity but you'll have to coordinate with the power realizer to buy enough power to support their heat plate. 

● No Gas cookers in the Forest, except within the Huts (2x2m) that can be placed by us or the shipping-containers

● Gas cookers outside the forest can only be operated when placed on a table (min 50 cm high, stable) and next to a fire-extinguisher (6kg ABC and valid) and a fire-blanket (to extinguish humans and oil). This applies for camps and freecamping.

● No barbecues or fires that are closer than 50m to the forest and contained in a Fire bowl or ton. They need to be guarded by an adult at all times and a fire-extinguisher (6kg ABC and valid) must be placed close to it.

● For Free Campers Kiez Burn may place a Tent, that fulfills the requirements, in the freecamping area where everyone can go and cook with their own gascookers. \nFree campers could also form and register low effort camps where only the kitchen setup is organised together.


:::tip
chopping, prepping and storage can be in a tent under normal conditions. 


Camps could also get electric stoves, if they can afford it/don´t have to feed to many. 

:::

 ● Bring enough food for your entire group for the duration of your stay. Dried, tinned, and other non-perishable food is recommended. Fresh fruits and vegetables will only keep for about 3 days.

● Snacks such as nuts, dried fruit, etc help keep you going and also make nice gifts

● Small water bottle to carry with you

​​​​​​​● Big canisters to carry water to your camp and to dispose of grey water

● Bring your own cup, bowl, cutlery, towel & washing gear, but avoid disposable items such as paper plates, etc. Bars and theme camps that gift food or drinks will expect you to bring your own plate, bowl, or cup.

● Bring hand sanitizer to clean your hands before you eat and after using the toilet.
