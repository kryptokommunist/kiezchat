## Description

We want to create the museum of emptiness, with the support of [xstage](https://xstageproject.com), a noncommercial Verein originally founded by TU physicists that has brought science communication and installations to festivals for over 10 years. With a team of three bringing three interactive art installations to Kiezburn — **Doyle**, **Bloom**, and **anattā**— and will be present throughout the festival to guide curious burners through the science, math, and philosophy behind each piece.

**Doyle** is a series of five laser-etched steel disks that animate when rotated. Using a geometric figure called a Doyle spiral — a mathematically precise circle packing — geometric patterns are engraved into polished steel at precise angles so that, as the disk turns, the reflections sequence like a zoetrope. The result is a mesmerising illusion: a static metal disk that appears to breathe and spiral. Each piece is unique. Visitors are invited to spin them by hand and watch the pattern come alive. The original piece was developed by a US artist that gave me permission to show a reverse engineered version. More at [kryptokommun.ist/portfolio/projects/tech/2025/11/01/doyle-packing.html](https://kryptokommun.ist/portfolio/projects/tech/2025/11/01/doyle-packing.html)

**anattā 𑀅𑀦𑀢𑁆𑀢𑀸** is an interactive two-way mirror installation for two people. "Anattā" means "non-self" in Buddhist tradition. Two people stand on opposite sides of a half-transparent mirror and align their faces — watching their reflections merge and dissolve into each other. Hand gestures detected by time-of-flight laser sensors let visitors blend the two reflections in real time, turning self and other into something fluid. It asks: *How does it feel to be me? How does it feel to be you? What remains when both categories dissolve?* More at <https://kryptokommun.ist/portfolio/projects/tech/2024/07/01/annata.html>

**Bloom** is a strobe light sculpture that explores the Golden Ratio in motion. 3D-printed sculptures — constructed using the same mathematical laws that govern plant growth — are set rotating under a synchronized strobe light. At each 137.5-degree turn (the Golden Angle), a flash fires, producing the illusion of an eternally growing, living flower. At rest it looks like an ordinary plastic form; under strobe light, magic unfolds. Originally conceived by John Edmark, this is our own built iteration. More at [xstageproject.com/experiments/bloom](https://xstageproject.com/experiments/bloom/)


Together these three installations form a contemplative corner where mathematics, nature, light, and identity meet. We'll be around for informal conversations, and experimenting throughout the festival.

## Where at Kiezburn would you like your Dream / Art to be?

We'd love a spot on the art plateau or near a high-foot-traffic path. The installations are compact — each Doyle disk is roughly 30–40 cm in diameter, Bloom needs a small table or stand, and anattā requires a hanging mechanism, rope or beam with space for two people to stand on either side. Total footprint approximately 15 - 30 m². Low ambient light would help both Bloom and anattā come alive at night. Also the installations lights should not disturb other art or spaces.

## Environmental Impact and LNT

**Doyle:** Steel disks are durable, reusable, and will not produce MOOP. No consumables. The only waste risk is packaging material during transport, which we will take home. Steel will last indefinitely and can be shown at future burns.

**Bloom:** 3D-printed PLA sculptures are solid, contained, and will not shed parts. The strobe unit is a sealed electronic device. All cabling will be secured and taped down. We will do a thorough sweep of our footprint on strike day.

**anattā:** The mirror frame is aluminium with 3D-printed parts. No consumables, no dyes, no MOOP risk. The LED strip and microcontroller are enclosed. All cabling secured. The installation packs down fully and leaves no trace.

No glitter, no dyes, no open flames, no toxic materials.

## Safety and Accessibility

**Doyle:** Purely mechanical — motor spun steel disks, speed is max 60rpm . Safe for all ages. The stands will be weighted and stable.

**Bloom:** The strobe light runs at \~60Hz which is within safe limits for most people.  The rotating sculpture (700rpm) is enclosed with a glas casing.

**anattā:** Low-voltage LED strip (5V) driven by a microcontroller. No heat, no exposed wiring. The mirror is framed and mounted in a stable aluminium frame. Time-of-flight sensors are low-power and eye-safe. The installation will be clearly anchored so it cannot tip.

Neither installation uses fire, high-powered lasers, or produces significant heat.
